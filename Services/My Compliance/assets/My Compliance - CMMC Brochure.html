The CMMC Challenge
DoD primes and subs know CMMC is coming to every contract — but CUI is scattered across systems, subcontractor flowdown is messy, and ITAR overlaps make cloud architecture genuinely hard. Generic IT vendors can’t help.
🎖

CMMC = No Contract Renewal

DoD contracts now require CMMC certification at award. Without Level 1 or Level 2 in place, existing contracts don’t renew and new bids get disqualified before technical evaluation.

Assessment-ready programs, not paperwork
🔍

CUI Data Mapping Chaos

Your CUI lives in emails, SharePoint, CAD drawings, ERP systems, and subcontractor portals. No one has mapped it — and you can’t protect what you haven’t scoped.

CUI scoping & data flow mapping

Subcontractor Flowdown

As a prime, you’re responsible for flowing DFARS requirements to subs. Subs are failing self-assessments and DIBCAC reviews — putting your primary contract at direct risk.

Prime-to-sub flowdown governance
🛡

ITAR + CMMC Overlap

ITAR requires U.S. persons and U.S. infrastructure. CMMC requires NIST 800-171 controls. Most cloud platforms don’t meet both without carefully architected enclaves.

GCC High & enclaved architecture

Why CMMC Now?

  • CMMC 2.0 rule is finalized — DoD enforcement is ramping through 2025-2026 with phased rollout across contract types and dollar thresholds
  • Prime contractors are aggressively flowing DFARS 7012/7019/7020/7021 requirements down to subs; sub failures are becoming prime contract failures
  • Microsoft GCC High, enclaved Azure, and DoD-grade tool stacks are now accessible to mid-market contractors — no more excuses on tooling
  • DIBCAC assessments are accelerating and self-assessment scores in SPRS are actively checked at contract award

CMMC Levels Explained
Three maturity tiers. Your required level is determined by the CUI sensitivity of the DoD program you support — and drives everything from controls to assessment cadence.
Level
1
Foundational

Foundational — FCI Only

Annual Self-Assessment · FAR 52.204-21

Baseline safeguarding for contractors who handle only Federal Contract Information (FCI) and no CUI. Covers 17 basic practices like access control, identification, and physical protection.

17 practices
Self-attested annually in SPRS
No CUI permitted
Level
2
Advanced

Advanced — Required for CUI

Triennial C3PAO Assessment · NIST SP 800-171

Full implementation of NIST SP 800-171 for any contractor handling CUI. Most defense contractors will need Level 2. Requires documented SSP, POA&M, and third-party assessment.

110 practices
Process maturity documented
C3PAO every 3 years

3

Expert — Highest-Priority Programs

Triennial Government Assessment · NIST SP 800-172

Enhanced protections against Advanced Persistent Threats for the most sensitive DoD programs. Adds a subset of NIST SP 800-172 enhanced practices on top of Level 2.

Engagement Models
Four ways to engage — from scoped fixed-fee assessments through retained vCISO leadership for the Defense Industrial Base.

CUI Scoping & Gap Assessment

Fixed-scope, fixed-fee engagement to scope CUI, run NIST 800-171 gap analysis, and deliver a remediation roadmap with cost estimate.

  • CUI & FCI data flow mapping
  • 110-control gap analysis with evidence review
  • Draft SSP skeleton & POA&M starter
  • SPRS scoring estimate & remediation roadmap
Best for: Contractors starting their CMMC program

Level 2 Implementation

End-to-end fixed-scope program to design, deploy, and document a full CMMC Level 2 environment over 6-12 months.

  • Enclave architecture (GCC High or equivalent)
  • Tool deployment: Passportal, Teramind, Veeam
  • Full SSP, POA&M, and evidence binder
  • C3PAO pre-assessment dry run
Best for: Contractors required to hit Level 2

CMMC Managed Program

Monthly retained program that keeps controls live, evidence current, and the organization assessment-ready year-round.

  • Monthly control health reviews
  • Evidence collection & retention
  • Incident response & DIBNet reporting support
  • Annual self-assessment refresh & SPRS update
Best for: Sustaining certification over time

vCISO for Defense Industrial Base

Retained virtual CISO with deep DIB experience — owning the CMMC program and flowing governance to subs and vendors.

  • Executive-level program ownership
  • Subcontractor flowdown governance
  • ITAR/EAR overlap advisory
  • DoD customer & C3PAO interface
