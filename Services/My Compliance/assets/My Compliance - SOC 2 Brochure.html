Why SOC 2 Now?

  • Enterprise buyers now require SOC 2 before contract execution — no report, no deal
  • Cyber insurance carriers increasingly require SOC 2 (or equivalent attestation) for coverage and renewal
  • The AICPA’s expanded Points of Focus have raised the evidentiary bar — old control playbooks no longer satisfy auditors
  • Type II is the de facto standard for enterprise procurement — Type I only buys you 6-12 months to get the real thing

SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria
Security (Common Criteria) is required in every SOC 2 report. The other four criteria are optional — we help you scope exactly what your customers, market, and risk profile require.

Security is Always In Scope

Every SOC 2 report covers the Common Criteria: access, change management, risk assessment, monitoring, and incident response.

Availability for SaaS with SLAs

Added when your contracts include uptime commitments or DR obligations. Evidence includes capacity planning, backup, and BCP testing.

Processing Integrity for Data Services

For platforms where completeness or accuracy of processing matters — FinTech, payroll, analytics, and transaction services.

Confidentiality & Privacy Add-Ons

Confidentiality protects customer IP and contracts. Privacy adds GDPR-adjacent PII controls — HealthTech and B2C platforms commonly scope both.
