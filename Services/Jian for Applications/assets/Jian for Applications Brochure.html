Why an app needs its own agent
Your RMM watches servers. Your SIEM watches security. Your backup tool watches backups. None of them can see a sync job silently freeze, or service lines billed in your system but never transmitted to the payer. That logic lives inside the application — so that’s where the agent has to live.
The blind spot
Generic monitoring stops at the app’s front door
  • A sync job exits “success” (exit 0) but copies zero rows — the dashboard stays green for months
  • Records added after a batch is sent are never transmitted — billed, never paid
  • Rejections pile up in a queue no alert is watching
  • The one person who understood it all has left the building
The fix
A dedicated agent that knows your app’s business logic
  • Reads your app’s own tables, queues & APIs — not just CPU and disk
  • Knows what “healthy” looks like — and what a revenue leak looks like
  • Traces any record end-to-end and produces the exact fix worklist
  • Documents the tribal knowledge so it never walks out the door again

Case Study — ORX
A revenue cycle, watched end-to-end
OrthoKinetix, powered by OrthoXpress is a California orthopedic DMEPOS biller. Revenue depends on claims being built, transmitted, accepted & posted across two payer worlds — commercial / Medicare via Waystar, and CA workers’-comp via DaisyBill. When the prior developer was off-boarded, Technijian took the platform end-to-end and put three Jian agents on it.
The data flow Jian watches
OXPLive

The Feed
~30-min sync job
→
OXPLiveNG

↴
Waystar / DaisyBill
837 clearinghouses → payers
The critical dependency: OXPLiveNG has no data of its own — it is entirely populated by the Feed. If the Feed stops, the app silently goes stale even while billing continues. That single failure is the root of almost every “claims aren’t showing” report — and exactly what Jian’s feed watchdog exists to catch.
Three agents on one revenue cycle
🔄
OXPLive Feed Watchdog Read-only
Compares OXPLiveNG against the OrthoXpressDB system of record — job & claim recency, source-vs-destination lag, and the open backfill gap. Traces any job or claim across both databases.
Catch: a silent 95-day feed outage once froze ~9,295 claims out of the management app. The watchdog now flags a freeze within 15 minutes.
📤
Waystar Clearinghouse Agent Read-only
Monitors 837 submission, 999 / 277CA / 835 responses and the REST API. Detects the batching leak (lines added after first batch, never sent), groups rejections into fix buckets, and traces any claim end-to-end.
Catch: ~1,615 service lines / ~$180K billed in the system but never transmitted — surfaced as a ready-to-resubmit worklist for the billing team.
🩺
DaisyBill 837 MCP Agent Read-only
Watches the live DaisyBill MCP server — bill-sync & EDI-837 recency, MCP + core-API health endpoints, and bill / payment counts — the CA workers’-comp half of the revenue cycle.
Status: 85 syncs / 82 EDI runs / 0 failures historically — now continuously health-checked for any >24h gap or 503.
Three agents, one revenue cycle, zero silent failures — every freeze, leak and rejection now has a watcher and a worklist.
