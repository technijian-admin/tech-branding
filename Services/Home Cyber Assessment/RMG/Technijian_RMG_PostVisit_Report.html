Decisions We Need From You

The following items are your decisions to make. We will support whichever path you choose, but a decision allows us to schedule the right work. No urgency required — your remote team will discuss each of these at the Month 1 call.

1. HikVision Camera Replacement — Recommended within 90 Days

This is our strongest recommendation from the visit. HikVision cameras are actively being targeted at internet scale due to known vulnerabilities. All four exterior cameras (front south, back north, front north, pool area) and a fifth patio camera are affected.

Even after we harden the current cameras (cloud relay off, firewall isolation, credentials changed), HikVision’s risk profile remains elevated due to the company’s ownership structure and its placement on the U.S. FCC Covered List. Our recommendation is to plan a replacement within 90 days.

What you need to decide: Would you like Technijian to recommend replacement cameras and coordinate with an installer, or do you have a preferred vendor or installer you’d like to use? We can work with either approach.

2. Network Segmentation (IoT SSID)

During the visit, you indicated you preferred a single WiFi network rather than separate Trusted/IoT/Guest segments. We understand the appeal — one network is simpler to manage day to day.

Our recommendation is still to create an IoT segment, for one specific reason: the HikVision cameras and NVR need to be isolated from your laptop and phones. Even after the cameras are replaced, the principle of putting surveillance equipment and smart TVs on a separate network from your financial and personal devices is a meaningful security improvement.

What you need to decide: Would you like us to set up a separate IoT-only SSID (hidden from normal view, so it’s not cluttering your WiFi list)? Or would you prefer we proceed only with isolating the cameras at the firewall level without creating additional SSIDs? Either approach is achievable once we have Orbi admin access back.

3. Brokerage and AT&T Account 2FA

Two accounts didn’t get 2FA enabled during the visit. Both are straightforward — the remote team can walk through either one in a 10-minute phone or screen-share session.

  • Brokerage account: Client preferred to handle this independently. If you’d like the remote team to walk through it with you, just let them know at the Month 1 call.
  • AT&T account: Login wasn’t available during the visit. Remote team can assist when you have AT&T account credentials available.
