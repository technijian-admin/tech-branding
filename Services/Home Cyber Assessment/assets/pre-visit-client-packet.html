What the Tech Will NOT Do

To set expectations clearly — this visit is focused on cybersecurity remediation. The following are outside today's scope:

  • Installing new hardware you haven't already agreed to (new routers, cameras, etc. — we'll recommend them, but install is a separate visit)
  • Physical camera replacement or alarm system replacement — those require specialist installers and separate scheduling
  • General computer tune-ups, printer troubleshooting unrelated to security, or help with software you use for work
  • Moving furniture, cable management, or drilling

If you have additional needs beyond the cybersecurity scope, let us know and we can schedule a follow-up visit — but we don't want to compromise today's schedule by trying to fit extra work into it.

4. What You Should NOT Do Before We Arrive

Please do not attempt to make any of the changes in the assessment report yourself before the visit. We've seen several cases where a well-intentioned early attempt (changing the WiFi password, for example) ended up disconnecting half the devices in the home, which then takes an hour of the visit to untangle.

Leave everything as it is. We'll do it together in the right order. The only thing we'd ask you to do in advance is locate the credentials and accounts listed above — not to change anything.

After the Visit: Your 24/7 Remote Support

A one-day onsite visit sets up the security baseline. Keeping it in good shape over time is where our 24/7 remote support comes in. This section explains what that looks like so there are no surprises.

How the Handoff Works

During the last hour of the onsite visit, your Technijian tech sets up a secure remote-access tool on your Dell XPS. This gives our 24/7 support team the ability to assist you without needing to drive to your house every time something needs attention — which is what makes it cost-effective and fast.

To be clear about privacy: the remote team has their own dedicated admin accounts on your network equipment — they do not use your personal credentials. Any remote session happens with your awareness (you'll see a notification), and sessions are logged.

Who Actually Helps You

Technijian operates with both a U.S. team (here in Orange County) and an international support team based in India, working on a 24/7 shift pattern. This is standard for modern managed services and is how we're able to offer overnight and weekend coverage at a reasonable price.

  • U.S.-based account management and escalations
  • International (India-based) Tier 1 and Tier 2 remote support — same team every time, not a rotating queue
  • Local dispatch available if something requires onsite hands — typically the same technician who did your initial visit

What the Remote Team Handles

The ongoing support covers most of what comes up in a home network over time:

  • Monthly patching — Windows updates on the laptop, firmware checks on router/mesh/cameras/printers
  • Backup verification — making sure your data is actually being backed up each month
  • Security log review — scanning for unusual device behavior on your network
  • Ad-hoc support — printer stopped working, a new device won't connect, a suspicious email you want to run past someone
  • Quarterly deep audit — a thorough review every three months
  • Annual re-assessment — a full refresh of the report we just did, once a year

What the Remote Team Does NOT Handle

Some things still require a local visit or specialist. The remote team will coordinate these for you, but they're billed separately:

  • Physical device replacement (cameras, alarm panel, printers)
  • Hardware failures that need onsite diagnosis
  • AT&T technician dispatches
  • Anything involving actual physical security of the home

Typical Monthly Time

For your specific environment (roughly 30 networked devices, two active users), we estimate about 7.5 hours per month of remote support work once things settle into a steady state. The first three months run a bit higher because the team is completing follow-up items from the onsite visit.

Your monthly retainer and exact terms are covered in a separate proposal — please review that document for pricing. If you have questions about the remote contract specifically, feel free to ask your Technijian account manager.
