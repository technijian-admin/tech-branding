Why Email Is Still the #1 Attack Vector
Email Security Hasn’t Kept Pace with the Threat

Every modern breach tour starts in the inbox. Attackers are using generative AI to write perfect phishing at scale, hiding payloads in QR codes, hijacking real conversations mid-thread, and weaponizing compromised accounts from the inside. Most legacy email gateways — and even Microsoft’s built-in defender — weren’t designed for any of this.

🎯
AI-Generated Phishing at Scale
Generative AI has cut the time to author a convincing phishing email from ~16 hours to ~5 minutes. Volume is up, quality is up, and keyword-based filters miss most of it.
🔒
Business Email Compromise
Wire-change requests, vendor impersonation, and payroll-redirect scams caused $3.05B in reported US losses in 2025 (FBI IC3). Legacy anti-spam has no signal to catch them.
📹
QR Code Phishing (“Quishing”)
QR codes inside PDFs and images bypass URL scanners. Users scan on their phone, skip corporate protections, and land on a credential-harvesting page.
👤
Compromised-Account Email
Once an employee’s Microsoft 365 account is taken over, every email they send is already “trusted.” Perimeter gateways never see it — only internal-mail protection does.
⚠️
Brand Forgery & Look-Alikes
Spoofed Microsoft, DocuSign, and bank emails look perfect to SPF/DKIM — because the domain checks pass. Only computer-vision AI catches the visual forgery.
😴
Security Awareness Fatigue
Annual SAT modules and quarterly phishing simulations don’t stick. Users need coaching in the moment they’re about to click — not months earlier in a slide deck.

Detection Methods & Coverage
How INKY Catches What Keyword Filters Miss

Traditional gateways score email on sender reputation, URLs, and keywords. Modern phishing looks clean on all three. INKY combines computer vision, generative-AI signals, and behavioral analysis to score what a message looks like, how it behaves, and whether it matches normal patterns for the sender and recipient — the way a trained analyst would.

📷 Computer-Vision Brand Forgery
INKY renders the email the way a user sees it and compares logos, layout, and typography against legitimate senders. Spoofed Microsoft, DocuSign, Wells Fargo, Chase, and hundreds more are caught visually — even when DKIM and SPF pass.
🧠 GenAI-Assisted Phishing Detection
LLM-authored phishing has perfect grammar and convincing tone — keyword filters lose. INKY uses language-model features plus conversational context to spot the signatures of AI-generated social engineering, including thread-hijack attacks.
📹 QR / Quishing Decoder
INKY decodes QR codes inline — even ones embedded in PDFs, PNGs, or screenshot attachments — and analyzes the destination URL before the user scans with their phone. QR phishing is one of the fastest-growing attack vectors since 2024.
👤 Account Takeover & Internal Phish
Once an M365 or Google account is compromised, outbound email carries full trust. INKY’s internal-mail module watches tenant-to-tenant messages for anomalous tone, timing, recipient patterns, and wire-change language — catching the attack from inside.
📬 BEC & Vendor Impersonation
Classic BEC — CEO wire requests, vendor invoice changes, payroll redirects — uses zero malware, so AV is useless. INKY scores behavioral signals: first-time sender + urgency + payment request = block or banner, regardless of domain reputation.
⛈ Post-Delivery Remediation
When threat intelligence updates after delivery (e.g. a URL weaponized hours later), INKY claws back the message from every inbox that received it — even if the user already opened it. No more “delete this email” IT broadcasts.
