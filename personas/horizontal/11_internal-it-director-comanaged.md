# 11. Sarah Okafor — Internal IT Director (Co-Managed IT Buyer)

**Archetype:** Head of IT / IT Director / VP IT at a 100 – 800 employee mid-market firm. She has a real internal IT team of 2 – 8 people. She does *not* want Technijian to replace them. She wants a co-managed partner to cover 24/7, specialist skills, and project burst. She's the technical peer — not a skeptic of MSPs, just allergic to condescension.
**Primary industry:** Cross-industry — wherever a 100 – 800 employee firm has grown an internal IT team but not to full self-sufficiency. Most common in manufacturing, distribution, pro services, healthcare networks, regional banks, mid-market SaaS
**Role in buying process:** Technical Buyer + Champion; budget controlled by her or shared with CFO/COO
**Priority tier:** Tier 1 — primary ICP

## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **24/7 coverage on a 9-to-6 team.** On-call rotation burns engineers. Turnover risk.
2. **Specialist skills she can't hire for.** Azure architect, M365/Security engineer, Kubernetes. FTE ROI unjustifiable.
3. **Project burst vs. BAU.** A migration eats 50% of her team for 6 months; everything else slows.
4. **Security tool fatigue.** 12 tools, 7 dashboards, 3 vendors alerting at different times.
5. **Vendor management sprawl.** 40+ vendors; contracts scattered.
6. **Board-ready reporting.** The CFO wants monthly metrics; she pieces them together manually.
7. **Shadow IT.** Departments sign SaaS contracts without her knowing.
8. **Help-desk volume** grows faster than headcount.
9. **On-call fatigue drives attrition** — she's replacing 1 – 2 engineers a year.
10. **CEO / CFO pressure to "do more with AI"** — she doesn't have time to evaluate.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- Burnout → key engineer quits during an ERP cutover.
- Ransomware → she's the face on the board slide.
- A project she championed fails visibly.
- CFO hires a consultant who recommends "outsourcing IT" because they don't see what her team does.
- A compliance audit finds gaps her team didn't flag.
- An MSP partnership that fails publicly.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- Nights/weekends on-call rotation.
- Part-time contractor engineer when needed.
- Documentation half-done in Confluence / OneNote.
- SIEM is "installed," tuning incomplete.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- No true 24/7 coverage.
- No on-demand specialists.
- No co-delivery cadence.
- No measurable reporting.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"I don't want a vendor replacing my team."**
   - **Why:** Core fear.
   - **Reframe:** Co-managed model is explicitly complementary. RACI with "IT Director remains directly accountable" at the top.

2. **"I've been burned by MSPs before."**
   - **Why:** Scar tissue.
   - **Reframe:** Ask what failed specifically; design RACI + delivery around those gaps; offer a 90-day pilot with defined exit.

3. **"Your pricing is too high for the coverage."**
   - **Why:** Comparing to hours-based MSPs.
   - **Reframe:** Bundled 24/7 + specialist access + SOC 2 posture of the MSP itself.

4. **"My team can learn Azure / Kubernetes."**
   - **Why:** Engineer pride.
   - **Reframe:** They can — on your dime, in 6 months, after 2 of them take certification classes. Or we bring the specialist today.

5. **"We don't want a long contract."**
   - **Why:** Flexibility needed.
   - **Reframe:** 12-month with 60-day convenience; option for month-to-month at premium.

6. **"How do you handle tool overlap with ours?"**
   - **Why:** Integration risk.
   - **Reframe:** Audit existing stack; integrate, not replace, unless replacement is business-justified.

7. **"What if your engineer and mine disagree on architecture?"**
   - **Why:** Real operational concern.
   - **Reframe:** Escalation RACI with her as tie-breaker; documented architecture-decision-record practice.

### Disqualifiers

- No internal IT team (she'd be persona 01 instead).
- CFO who has decided to outsource IT entirely (co-managed won't fit).
- Internal team hostile to MSPs by default (cultural mismatch).

## 20. Risks & Red Flags for Technijian
