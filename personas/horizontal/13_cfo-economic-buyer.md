# 13. Terry Williams — CFO / Controller (Economic Buyer & Signatory)

**Archetype:** CFO, Controller, or VP Finance at a 50 – 800 employee mid-market firm across verticals. Sign any IT / security / compliance contract above the CEO/owner's delegated threshold. ## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **Cyber insurance renewal pain.** Questionnaires growing, premiums rising, underwriters demanding controls he doesn't have.
2. **Audit evidence.** Auditors ask for IT-control evidence he has to chase.
3. **IT spend transparency.** Sprawl of SaaS, vendors, licenses; hard to optimize.
4. **Business continuity.** A week-long outage would take 6 – 12 months of revenue to recover from. No tested DR.
5. **Ransomware's financial reality.** $1M – $5M average small-to-mid breach cost. Not hypothetical anymore.
6. **Compliance-triggered spend surprise.** PE sponsor mandates SOC 2 or HIPAA; IT Director asks for $300K he didn't budget.
7. **PE quarterly reporting.** Sponsor wants cyber maturity metrics alongside financial.
8. **M&A diligence.** Buyer or seller uncovers IT mess in diligence — deal risk.
9. **Shadow IT CC charges.** Can't reconcile credit card line items to SaaS tools.
10. **IT vs finance tension.** IT wants a capital refresh; finance wants to defer.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- Breach that triggers material weakness in audit.
- Insurance denial of a cyber claim.
- PE sponsor questioning financial stewardship.
- Restatement of financials.
- M&A deal breaking on IT diligence.
- Ransomware + no backups + no insurance + cash crisis.
- Board asking a question he can't answer.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- Annual risk assessment via consulting firm.
- Insurance broker handles renewal questionnaire "together."
- IT Director provides evidence upon request.
- Budget IT at last year +5%.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- No monthly cyber / IT risk reporting.
- No tested DR / BCP.
- No vendor-risk program.
- No integrated audit-evidence workflow.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"This wasn't in the budget."**
   - **Why:** Real — CFOs guard budget.
   - **Reframe:** Tie to audit / insurance / compliance / M&A requirement — ROI vs non-compliance cost.

2. **"Cheaper alternatives exist."**
   - **Why:** Default framing.
   - **Reframe:** TCO comparison including breach-cost math, insurance-premium impact, evidence-workflow time saved.

3. **"Why a 3-year commitment?"**
   - **Why:** Flexibility.
   - **Reframe:** 12-month with renewal options; no 3-year unless he wants the discount.

4. **"Can I get a break-fee clause?"**
   - **Why:** Risk-transfer.
   - **Reframe:** Yes — termination for cause + convenience with transition SOW.

5. **"I want to see your SOC 2 / insurance certificates."**
   - **Why:** Standard governance.
   - **Reframe:** Provide SOC 2 Type II + certificates of insurance.

6. **"Can we pilot first?"**
   - **Why:** De-risk.
   - **Reframe:** 90-day pilot priced fixed-fee with committed success criteria.

7. **"The IT Director pushed this; why should I sign?"**
   - **Why:** CFO skepticism of IT-driven spend.
   - **Reframe:** Frame in audit / insurance / board terms, not IT.

### Disqualifiers

- CFO hostile to IT spend under any circumstances.
- CFO who disbelieves cyber risk.
- CFO in organizations with imminent bankruptcy risk.
