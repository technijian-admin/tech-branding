## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **Advisor text-message compliance.** Advisors use personal phones, iMessage, WhatsApp. JP Morgan paid $200M for this. Brian's firm has a policy; adherence is spotty. Archiving reality is incomplete.
2. **AI governance is undefined.** Advisors are pasting client info into ChatGPT. He knows it's happening. No tooling stops it. No policy is enforced in real time.
3. **Vendor risk program is manual.** Spreadsheet of 60 – 120 vendors. SOC 2 renewals scattered in inboxes. Tier 1 reviews are overdue.
4. **Clearing firm outages.** Schwab / Fidelity / LPL go down and advisors can't trade. Technically not his IT — but he gets the angry calls.
5. **Email + file-sharing compliance.** Advisors share statements via personal Gmail "for convenience." DLP catches some of it.
6. **Reg S-P 72-hour clock.** 2024 amendments tightened breach notification timelines. His current MSP doesn't have a documented 72-hour playbook.
7. **Social media / external communications.** Advisors post on LinkedIn, record podcasts, do webinars. Each is a reviewable communication under FINRA Rule 2210. Capture + review is painful.
8. **Advisor turnover & offboarding.** Terminated advisor takes client list + residual communications. Offboarding is non-atomic.
9. **Books & records portability.** If the firm is acquired or merges, records need to move intact with a full audit trail. He's not confident.
10. **Incident response + insurance timing.** Cyber incident → first call is insurance carrier; second call is outside counsel; third is IT. Need a coordinated playbook, not ad-hoc.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- 8210 letter from FINRA.
- Enforcement action with consent / censure / fine on his record.
- A Reg S-P violation during the 72-hour clock.
- Ransomware encrypting all archived records and backups together.
- An advisor discovered to have run client data through ChatGPT, creating a privacy breach.
- SEC exam pulls a sample email that doesn't exist in archiving.
- Cyber insurance denies a claim for "failure to implement reasonable controls."

### Current workarounds / status quo

- Quarterly manual sample review of communications.
- Spreadsheet vendor risk register with dates.
- "Don't use ChatGPT for client info" email to all advisors last year (unenforced).
- MSP runs EDR, SIEM is "in progress."
- IR plan dated 2022, never tabletop-tested.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- Generic MSP doesn't speak FINRA.
- Archiving vendor (Smarsh / Global Relay) is separate silo from IT.
- Cyber insurance broker sends questionnaire; MSP fills half of it inaccurately.
- No AI-interaction audit layer.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"Do you have BD/RIA references? Name three."**
   - **Why:** Financial services compliance is its own world.
   - **Reframe:** 3 peer CCO references with phone numbers, matched on firm type.

2. **"How do you integrate with Smarsh / Global Relay?"**
   - **Why:** Archiving must not regress.
   - **Reframe:** Direct integration stories + RACI with archiving vendor.

3. **"Can you handle a 72-hour Reg S-P breach notification?"**
   - **Why:** New rule; most MSPs don't know it.
   - **Reframe:** Documented 72-hour playbook + coordination template with outside counsel + carrier.

4. **"What about AI governance?"**
   - **Why:** New frontier.
   - **Reframe:** Chat.AI Financial Services — FINRA/SEC-aware multi-model AI with WORM retention, audit trails for every AI interaction, advisor-specific governance.

5. **"My outsourced CCO firm already does half of this."**
   - **Why:** Turf concerns.
   - **Reframe:** Technijian complements the CCO firm — they do governance/policy, we do technical controls + monitoring. Explicit RACI.

6. **"We're in acquisition talks and can't commit."**
   - **Why:** Legitimate freeze.
   - **Reframe:** 90-day readiness project only; full rollout post-close. Use the pause to do due diligence prep.

7. **"Pricing looks high."**
   - **Why:** Benchmarking against hours-MSP without the compliance overlay.
   - **Reframe:** Cost of one enforcement action vs annualized spend. NSCP enforcement stats.

### Disqualifiers

- <10 advisor firm — below threshold, refer to 01.
- Insurance-only / life-insurance-only firm — compliance regime is different (not our sweet spot).
- Firm in active FINRA enforcement — delivery risk, adverse timing.

## 20. Risks & Red Flags for Technijian

- **Enforcement-adjacent risk.** If Technijian misses a breach notice timeline, we're in the consent order blast radius. Strict playbook + annual drill required.
- **Offshore support disclosure.** Some firms disclose vendor offshore access to clients. Route support through U.S. analysts for these accounts.
- **Advisor-owned shadow tools.** We can't govern everything; make scope explicit.
- **M&A pause risk.** If firm is acquired mid-contract, decisions freeze 60 – 120 days. Plan the pipeline.
- **Enforcement publication.** If a client ever gets hit, it becomes public; Technijian could be named in press. Strong MSA indemnification + PR protocol.
