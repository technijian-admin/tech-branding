## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **The one-person MSP problem.** Current "IT guy" is a solo operator who goes on vacation / gets sick / stops responding. When the server goes down, there's no backup. Mark has learned by experience that one-person IT is single-point-of-failure.
2. **No one can say what they have.** No asset inventory, no diagram, no password list in one place. The one-person MSP has it all "in his head." If that person leaves, so does institutional knowledge.
3. **Insurance questionnaires keep getting longer.** Every renewal the broker sends a 3-page cyber questionnaire. Mark fakes answers because he doesn't actually know.
4. **Employees waste time on IT stuff.** Office manager spends 6 – 10 hours/week on IT tickets. Revenue-generating employees sometimes do their own IT triage.
5. **Backup isn't really tested.** Someone set up backups years ago. Mark has never seen a restore test. He assumes it works because "we haven't had a problem."
6. **Email & file sharing is a patchwork.** Dropbox + Gmail + a local server + OneDrive — no one knows the source of truth.
7. **Compliance creep.** A customer or insurance carrier is starting to ask about SOC 2 or HIPAA-ish questions and Mark doesn't know what to say.
8. **Shadow spend.** Can't tell you what his company spends on software and SaaS. It just hits the card.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- Ransomware that hits payroll week — cash crisis plus customer-trust crisis.
- The one-person MSP disappears and no one knows any passwords.
- A lawsuit over a data incident he couldn't document.
- His kid comes into the business and inherits a mess.
- A better-run competitor moves on a big customer during an outage.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- Office manager keeps a notebook of passwords.
- Backup "runs at night, I think."
- Two laptops older than 6 years still in service because nobody re-imaged them.
- Employees use personal Gmail for large file transfers.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- No 24/7 coverage.
- No monthly reporting — just invoices.
- No strategic conversations (roadmap, budget, risk).
- Reactive only — nothing proactive.
- No security posture whatsoever beyond "we have antivirus."

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"It's too expensive — my current guy is $X."**
   - **Why:** He's comparing retainer-only pricing to all-in managed IT.
   - **Reframe:** "Your current guy is $X and your office manager spends 8 hours a week on IT. That's another $Y in hidden cost. We replace both."

2. **"I don't want to sign a long-term contract."**
   - **Why:** Burned before by auto-renewing vendors.
   - **Reframe:** 60-day out-clause. 90-day stabilization period. "If it's not working at day 90, we part as friends."

3. **"I trust my current IT guy — he's been with us forever."**
   - **Why:** Relationship debt. Guilt about replacing a long-time vendor.
   - **Reframe:** Offer co-managed option. Keep the trusted person for relationship continuity; add Technijian's depth behind them.

4. **"I don't understand half of what you're saying."**
   - **Why:** MSP reps often over-jargon. He checks out.
   - **Reframe:** Switch to outcome language: "uptime," "no 2 AM calls," "you can document what you have for an insurance form."

5. **"Why should I believe Technijian is different from the last MSP?"**
   - **Why:** Scar tissue.
   - **Reframe:** Dedicated pod model (same team every time), 92+ CSAT number, local references with phone numbers.

6. **"I'll think about it."**
   - **Why:** Polite deflection when he isn't sold but isn't going to argue.
   - **Reframe:** Offer a zero-commitment IT Assessment ($0 – $3,500 fixed-fee) to create a reason for a follow-up.

### Disqualifiers

- Multi-national or high-tech SaaS — he'll feel oversold and we'll under-serve.
- Already has in-house IT director — refer to persona 11 instead.
- Regulated industry with hard HIPAA/FINRA/CMMC — refer to personas 02/03/04.

## 17. Anti-Persona / Not a Fit

### Looks like Mark but isn't a buyer

- **Pre-revenue startup founder** — no budget, will haggle the floor out

- Revenue under $3M
- Already has an internal IT director (go to persona 11)

- Hostile to references; refuses to let us talk to his CPA or peers

- Refuses to share an employee count or org chart

## 20. Risks & Red Flags for Technijian

- **Expectations drift.** Mark sold on "one call for everything" — will ask us to fix printers, LOB apps we don't support, personal-email accounts. Need a clear scope document and a disciplined account manager.
- **Over-reliance on office manager.** If she leaves, knowledge leaves. Mitigation: document at our side, share runbook back to customer.
- **Slow decisions on capital items.** He'll approve OpEx quickly but drag on a $40K server refresh for 6 months. Budget conversations at QBR.
- **Personal relationship overweight.** If the assigned account lead leaves Technijian, Mark's loyalty follows the person, not the firm. Mitigation: pod introduces 2 – 3 team members early, not just the account owner.
