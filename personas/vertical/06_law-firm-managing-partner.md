## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **Attorneys use personal email for large transfers** ("M365 attachment size blocked it"). Client-confidential data leaks constantly.
2. **DMS is a nightmare.** Old iManage / Worldox / NetDocuments with 20 years of documents; matter-numbering inconsistent; search is broken; attorneys save to desktop instead.
3. **Client security questionnaires.** Corporate clients require 80-question security addenda. Helena cobbles answers with Catherine and outside IT vendor.
4. **Laterals arrive with their book in a mess.** Files on a personal hard drive, emails in a PST, contacts in LinkedIn. Ingesting is 30 days and half-done.
5. **Trust accounting anxiety.** A wire-fraud incident at a peer firm → Helena is one bad email away from the same.
6. **AI use is un-governed.** Partners paste opposing-counsel briefs into ChatGPT; associates generate memos with Copilot. Ethics opinions are behind the behavior.
7. **Remote / hybrid attorney work** has created 4 years of endpoint drift. Some laptops haven't been touched by IT since 2021.
8. **Conflicts checks** go across systems manually; new-matter intake is slower than it should be.
9. **MP's own laptop.** Catherine's laptop is precious and often the slowest.
10. **eDiscovery vendor handoffs.** Data exports to Relativity / Everlaw are always painful.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- Wire-fraud client loss → malpractice claim → Bar referral.
- Ransomware encrypting DMS mid-trial.
- Client data leak → matter-closure → public news.
- Associate uses AI in a way that hallucinates a brief (à la Avianca/Mata v Avianca) and ends up sanctioned.
- Lateral partner's old firm triggers a conflicts / confidentiality issue.
- OCCO or State Bar inquiry into IT security posture.
- A partner's personal breach (home network) that exposes firm email.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- ShareFile / Dropbox Business / OneDrive for client transfers (inconsistent).
- "Don't use ChatGPT with client info" email sent last year.
- IT coordinator onboards laterals manually over 2 – 4 weeks.
- Trust-accounting wire verification protocol enforced on paper.
- Conflict check is Excel + manual email.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- MSP doesn't speak ABA rules / attorney ethics.
- No 24/7 SOC.
- No DMS hygiene project.
- No AI governance layer.
- No structured security questionnaire response.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"Our DMS vendor says to use their IT partner only."**
   - **Why:** DMS vendors push their partners.
   - **Reframe:** Technijian has done 10+ NetDocuments / iManage engagements. Provide RACI; stay lane-clean.

2. **"The partners will never agree to change."**
   - **Why:** Consensus-driven culture.
   - **Reframe:** Pilot with 1 practice group + a champion partner.

3. **"How do you handle confidentiality if a tech sees a privileged document?"**
   - **Why:** ABA Model Rule 1.6 duty.
   - **Reframe:** NDA + confidentiality addendum; PHI/PI minimization training; read-only support mode; logged access.

4. **"Our current MSP is cheaper."**
   - **Why:** Hours-based comparison.
   - **Reframe:** Show total cost including attorney-hour leakage to IT issues.

5. **"We don't want to move to the cloud."**
   - **Why:** Client-data residency fears.
   - **Reframe:** Hybrid — My Private Cloud + M365; data residency documented; compliance aligned.

6. **"What about AI?"**
   - **Why:** Genuine concern + curiosity.
   - **Reframe:** AI governance policy + sanctioned-tool pilot (e.g., Harvey / Copilot with DLP). Chat.AI for legal research with audit trail.

7. **"We already have iManage / NetDocuments support."**
   - **Why:** Turf.
   - **Reframe:** Don't replace DMS vendor; complement at IT-infrastructure layer.

### Disqualifiers

- Solo practitioner or <15-attorney firm — below our economics.
- Firm in active malpractice litigation over IT — delivery risk.
- Partnership culture hostile to vendor spend — long painful cycle.
