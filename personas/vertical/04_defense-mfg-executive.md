# 04. Greg Mahoney — Defense Manufacturer COO / VP Operations

**Archetype:** COO or VP of Operations at a precision manufacturer in the Defense Industrial Base (DIB). Runs CNC / fab / machining or precision assembly. Lives with ITAR, AS9100, CMMC 2.0, DFARS 7012, NIST 800-171, and an ERP from 1998.

## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **CMMC 2.0 Level 2.** 110 NIST 800-171 controls. Greg's current MSP can't even read the control list. Assessment window closing. Primes are starting to require certification for new awards.
2. **CUI scoping.** He doesn't actually know where CUI lives — is it in ERP, in CAD, in emails, on shop floor tablets? Scoping is the first 30 days of any CMMC project.
3. **Legacy ERP can't be patched.** Runs on Windows Server 2012, SQL 2008. IT says "don't touch it." Compliance says "patch or segment."
4. **ITAR deemed exports.** Foreign national employees, foreign suppliers, cloud vendors — each is a deemed-export question. Currently handled "carefully" (read: anxiously).
5. **No real IT partner.** Current MSP handles tickets. No strategic guidance. No CMMC language at all.
6. **Physical + digital convergence.** CNC machines, PLCs, test equipment — all IP-connected, most unpatched, some with defaults. ICS/OT security is a yawn-to-them topic until an auditor raises it.
7. **Prime flowdown questionnaires.** Every new contract comes with a 40-page cyber/compliance questionnaire. Greg's team fills them out on deadline, not accurately.
8. **DFARS 7012 reporting clock.** 72 hours from discovery. No playbook. First incident will be a fire drill.
9. **Backup + DR never tested.** Ransomware would halt production.
10. **Talent cost.** Hiring a CISO or cyber staffer is $200K+ and unjustifiable for a 80-person shop.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- Lose a prime contract because of CMMC non-compliance.
- DoD IG or DCSA notice.
- ITAR violation with fines in the hundreds of thousands to millions.
- Ransomware shuts the floor down for a week mid-program.
- A nation-state actor actually targets the firm — it happens.
- AS9100 audit finds an IT control gap and escalates to a major.
- Employee opens a phishing email and compromises the CUI enclave.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- "We think we're isolated" network.
- Shared domain admin password written down.
- Nights/weekends patching when the floor is down.
- MSP promises CMMC "help" but it's a $15K document review.
- Spreadsheet of CMMC controls; most are "in progress" status.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- MSP doesn't speak CMMC / NIST 800-171.
- No SIEM, no SOC.
- No documented SSP (System Security Plan) — required for CMMC.
- No evidence workflow.
- No ICS/OT visibility.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"CMMC is going to kill us. We can't afford it."**
   - **Why:** Sticker shock; small shop economics.
   - **Reframe:** CMMC failure costs the contract. Show phased pricing; show subsidies / prime program offsets where available. Reference case of peer who lost work.

2. **"GCC High is too expensive and hard."**
   - **Why:** True — it is harder and pricier than commercial M365.
   - **Reframe:** For firms with CUI, it's the only path to NIST 800-171 AC/SC/SI coverage. Cost is roughly 2x commercial but unavoidable. We've done it 10+ times.

3. **"Our current MSP says they can do CMMC."**
   - **Why:** MSPs overstate CMMC capability.
   - **Reframe:** Ask for their SSP template, their POA&M methodology, and one DIB reference. Usually ends the conversation.

4. **"We'll just do the self-assessment."**
   - **Why:** L1 allows self-attestation; L2 does not for prioritized acquisitions.
   - **Reframe:** Review the prime's flowdown language. Usually explicitly says C3PAO-certified L2.

5. **"Our CEO doesn't believe cyber is real."**
   - **Why:** Generational / cultural gap.
   - **Reframe:** Show the Solar Winds / MOVEit / Colonial Pipeline case. Show CMMC contract-loss case studies. Board-grade language.

6. **"How long will this take?"**
   - **Why:** Deadline panic.
   - **Reframe:** 8 – 14 months typical for a 50 – 100 person shop; longer if legacy ERP stays. Honest timeline + phased plan.

7. **"Can we keep our legacy ERP?"**
   - **Why:** ERP replacement is an existential project.
   - **Reframe:** Segment, don't replace. ERP lives in its own enclave; compensating controls; documented risk acceptance.

### Disqualifiers

- Firm with no CUI (L1 only) — don't oversell.
- Firm unwilling to invest ~4 – 8% of revenue in compliance remediation.
- Owner in denial about CMMC timeline.

## 20. Risks & Red Flags for Technijian

- **CMMC assessment dependency.** We don't issue certs; a C3PAO does. Scope our delivery accurately or we take blame for a cert we don't control.
- **Legacy ERP segmentation.** Compensating controls deserve board-level risk acceptance documentation.
- **ITAR deemed exports.** Any Technijian non-US-citizen accessing ITAR data is a violation. Route accounts to US-citizen staff only; contract explicitly.
- **Prime program optics.** If we cause a delay, prime blames the sub who blames us. Strong communication protocol.
- **Shop-floor downtime.** ERP / domain cutover risk halts production. Always do these off-shift.
- **DSS / DCSA visit.** If a facility is cleared, our staff access is scrutinized. Follow protocol.
