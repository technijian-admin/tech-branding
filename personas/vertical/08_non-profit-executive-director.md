## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **Old hardware everywhere.** Staff on 6 – 10 year old donated PCs. Laptops refresh only when one dies.
2. **Donor database is a mess.** Years of imports, duplicates, merged records. Fundraising hates it.
3. **No one owns IT.** The "IT person" is either a volunteer board member, a part-time IT coordinator, or the MSP's junior tech.
4. **Grant-compliance docs are scattered.** Audit prep = 3 weeks of scavenger hunt.
5. **Phishing is rampant.** Staff aren't trained; volunteers have access; donors get spoofed.
6. **Remote / hybrid drift.** COVID-era patterns left 20 – 40% of staff remote; endpoints untouched since.
7. **Online giving friction** — donate page abandonment high.
8. **Tech purchases need board justification** — every decision is a 3-month conversation.
9. **Community health specific:** EHR integrations, grant-funded services, HIPAA overlay on a thin budget.
10. **Education specific:** student-data privacy (FERPA), parent-portal integrations, SIS problems.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- Donor data breach → reputation crisis → fundraising collapse.
- Audit finding / grant clawback.
- Ransomware during a campaign.
- A major donor discovers lax controls after a breach.
- Cyber insurance denial of claim for "failure to implement reasonable controls."
- Board discovers she doesn't know enough about IT risk.
- Program staff email accessed by unauthorized party exposing client info.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- TechSoup for software licenses.
- "We use Google Drive for everything" or "SharePoint is too confusing."
- Shared spreadsheets for donor mail-merge.
- Ad-hoc backup, if any.
- Free Mailchimp / Constant Contact.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- No 24/7 support — staff waits till Monday.
- No cybersecurity training that sticks.
- No donor-privacy posture documented.
- No M365 Non-Profit SKU optimization.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"We can't afford it."**
   - **Why:** Reality of non-profit economics.
   - **Reframe:** Non-profit pricing; TechSoup discounts; phased implementation; in-kind / pro-bono possible components.

2. **"My board will never approve this."**
   - **Why:** Board buy-in needed.
   - **Reframe:** Prepare board-ready 1-page brief + risk narrative (donor-data protection, audit-ready).

3. **"We have a volunteer who does our IT."**
   - **Why:** Volunteer dependency.
   - **Reframe:** Complement the volunteer; they get strategic support from Technijian.

4. **"Can we do just one piece?"**
   - **Why:** Smaller commitment bias.
   - **Reframe:** Start with M365 Non-Profit cleanup + MFA ($8K – $15K project) as a clear quick win.

5. **"We want a non-profit-dedicated provider."**
   - **Why:** Sector solidarity.
   - **Reframe:** Technijian has case studies in non-profit (Cross-Industry, Non-Profit folders), and brings SMB + compliance depth most sector-only vendors lack.

### Disqualifiers

- <5 staff all-volunteer org — too small.
- Political-advocacy-only 501(c)(4) high-risk.
- Org in deficit operations — delivery risk.
