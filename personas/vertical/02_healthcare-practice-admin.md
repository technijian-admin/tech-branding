# 02. Angela Santos — Healthcare Practice Administrator

**Archetype:** Non-physician Practice Administrator (or COO / Director of Operations) running a multi-site specialty medical group, multi-physician primary care, or ambulatory surgical center. Lives at the intersection of HIPAA, EHR uptime, and payroll.
**Primary industry:** Healthcare — multi-site specialty practice, medical group, ASC, behavioral health, dental chain, med spa with medical director
**Role in buying process:** Economic Buyer + Champion (bundles the decision for the physician-owner partnership)
**Priority tier:** Tier 1 — primary ICP

## 4. Goals

### Business goals (12-month horizon)

1. **Zero HIPAA breaches** / reportable incidents.
2. Successful insurance renewal without the cyber questionnaire blowing up the premium.
3. EHR uptime >99.9% during clinic hours.
4. Phone system reliability across all sites (no rolls to voicemail during clinic).
5. Prep for a SOC 2 or third-party risk questionnaire if the practice is in acquisition talks.
6. Get off paper for a few remaining workflows (intake, referrals).

## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **EHR goes down during clinic and no one can document.** Patients in rooms, staff on paper, no one knows when it'll be back, ticket queue silent. Once a quarter event.
2. **The OCR nightmare scenario.** Email compromise of a staff account → PHI in the sent folder → Angela realizes she owes a breach notification and doesn't know the clock.
3. **Ransomware anxiety.** She reads about another healthcare org paying $3M ransom, checks her backups, gets told "we're good" — doesn't actually know.
4. **HIPAA training on autopilot.** Staff clicks through SkillSoft modules; no one knows if anyone actually learned anything. Phishing simulation scores are bad.
5. **M365 licensing & mailbox mess.** Terminated staff keep mail access for weeks. Physicians log in from personal devices. Shadow IT on personal Gmail.
6. **Multi-site coordination.** Printer at the Newport site, phones at the Irvine site, internet at the Long Beach site — three tickets, three vendors, zero coordination.
7. **Payer contract risk.** Bigger payers send supply-chain cyber questionnaires; she answers as best she can and hopes.
8. **Physician partners want Chat.AI / ambient scribe / AI scheduling** and she has to figure out how to deploy safely under HIPAA.
9. **EHR integrations.** Lab, radiology, scheduling, revenue cycle — each has a separate project, each requires an IT resource she doesn't have.
10. **On-call phone coverage.** After-hours physician calls get routed to the wrong answering service. She fields angry physician texts at 10 PM.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- OCR complaint investigation, especially if it results in a public Resolution Agreement.
- Clinical outage during a surgical case.
- A physician partner discovers a cyber insurance gap during a board meeting.
- Ransomware paralyzing the practice for 3+ days.
- A whistleblower complaint about HIPAA practices that went unaddressed.
- A key billing or scheduling staffer quits in the middle of a system change.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- Email-compromise playbook? Ad-hoc. Calls current MSP and hopes.
- Backups "tested" by restoring one file annually.
- BAAs signed but not tracked; no inventory of who has what data.
- Physicians' personal devices allowed on guest WiFi without MDM.
- One spreadsheet for vendor contact list, one for asset inventory (both out of date).

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- MSP doesn't speak HIPAA fluently.
- No 24/7 MDR — alerts just aren't responded to after hours.
- No one proactively walks through the OCR audit controls quarterly.
- EHR vendor and MSP blame each other when integrations break.
- No evidence-capture discipline — she has no folder ready for an audit.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"Do you have healthcare-specific clients / references?"**
   - **Why:** HIPAA nuance is real; she's been burned by generic MSPs.
   - **Reframe:** 2 – 3 peer practice admin references by phone, in her specialty if possible. Reference Case Studies/Healthcare case studies.

2. **"Our EHR vendor won't let anyone else touch the integration."**
   - **Why:** EHR vendors own the integration moat.
   - **Reframe:** Technijian coordinates — doesn't replace the EHR vendor. Explicit RACI on who owns what.

3. **"Can you handle a breach notification?"**
   - **Why:** She's worried about legal clock management.
   - **Reframe:** Playbook + IR retainer + healthcare attorney partner relationship + template BAA-aligned breach notification package.

4. **"The physicians won't tolerate a disruption."**
   - **Why:** Change management is existential in a partnership.
   - **Reframe:** Wave-based migration during clinic downtime; dry-run weekends; hypercare periods; physician-specific concierge support for the first 30 days.

5. **"My current MSP is cheaper."**
   - **Why:** Apples-to-oranges comparison against hours-based MSP.
   - **Reframe:** Total cost including breach risk, downtime hours, compliance evidence time. Show three-year TCO.

6. **"I can't run a change during [conference / year-end / flu season]."**
   - **Why:** Legitimate calendar constraints.
   - **Reframe:** Read the calendar. Start with readiness & BAA work while waiting for a deployment window.

7. **"HIPAA is just a checkbox for us."**
   - **Why:** Denial — she knows it's not, but selling the physicians is hard.
   - **Reframe:** Show a redacted OCR Resolution Agreement from a peer-sized practice ($1 – $3M fines are common). Let the evidence speak.

### Disqualifiers

- Practice with <10 employees — overkill, refer to 01.
- Single-site, single-provider — overkill.
- Hospital-owned practice — hospital IT makes the decisions; Angela doesn't have buying power.
- Anti-change physician partnership ("we've always done it this way") — long, painful sales cycle.

- **Healthcare-specific references** (MGMA members, specialty society peers)

- AI-readiness guidance (Chat.AI Healthcare)

### Vendor-selection priorities (ranked)

1. HIPAA + healthcare experience
2. Incident response capability (15-minute SLA resonates)
3. Reference strength — specialty match > size match
4. Responsiveness during discovery (she's testing)
5. Pricing clarity
6. Local presence (she wants us on-site if something goes wrong)
7. EHR vendor working relationship

### What they're skeptical of by default

- Generic "healthcare-compliant cloud" marketing without BAA specifics
- Promises of "guaranteed HIPAA compliance" (violates her own training)
- Offshore-only support (even if labeled 24/7)
- Non-RACI'd engagements that blur lines with EHR vendor

## 20. Risks & Red Flags for Technijian

- **Overselling HIPAA.** Never say "guaranteed compliant." Always say "navigate HIPAA" (per brand guide). Overpromises become legal exposure.
- **EHR vendor finger-pointing.** Integrations break; make sure RACI is explicit so Technijian isn't left holding the bag.
- **PHI leakage during support.** Screen-share with PHI visible — every tech needs PHI-minimization training.
- **Slow payment in PE-backed practices.** PE AP cycles are 60 – 90 days. Account planning should assume it.
- **Lawsuit exposure.** Deliver IR in coordination with healthcare counsel; don't freelance.
- **Scope creep on credentialing / HR systems.** Those aren't in Technijian's wheelhouse; refer out.
