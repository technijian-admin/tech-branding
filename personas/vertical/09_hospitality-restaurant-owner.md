## 5. Pain Points & Challenges

### Top operational pains (ranked)

1. **Google Maps / SEO / review mgmt** is fragmented. Each location has its own profile, inconsistent info, review responses erratic.
2. **PCI compliance** is a mystery. POS vendor says "we're PCI-compliant" — Antonio assumes he is too. He isn't.
3. **Wi-Fi and network across units** — guests on the same network as POS terminals (segmentation absent).
4. **POS outages during peak** — Friday 7 PM rush, POS dies, paper tickets, chaos.
5. **Delivery-platform tablets proliferate** — DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub each a tablet. Consolidation tools exist; he's behind.
6. **Turnover IT cost** — every new GM needs access to 8 systems; happens every 4 months.
7. **Ransomware / phishing** — he's heard of restaurant groups hit; never thinks it'll be him.
8. **Loyalty data privacy** — if Toast's loyalty database leaks, who's liable? He isn't sure.
9. **Private-event lead capture** is manual — inquiries come in via email, lost in inbox.
10. **Catering operations** — staffing, inventory, delivery — mostly managed in spreadsheets.

### Biggest fears / worst outcomes

- POS down during Saturday night rush (real).
- Credit-card breach → fines, class action, PR damage.
- Google Maps listing hijacked / review bombed.
- Health-dept or labor-dept fine tied to an IT / records issue.
- Delivery platforms raise commissions again.
- A key GM walks with proprietary recipes.

### Current workarounds / status quo

- Marketing director handles reviews (inconsistently).
- POS vendor does PCI at "merchant level" only — his controls are undocumented.
- Office MSP is not in the restaurants.
- GM training packet in a shared Google Drive.

### Gaps in current providers / tools

- No SEO program — hoping Yelp + Google My Business run themselves.
- No network segmentation between POS and guest Wi-Fi.
- No corporate-side security training.
- No incident playbook.

## 9. Objections & Pushbacks

1. **"My POS vendor does all this."**
   - **Why:** Belief that POS covers ops.
   - **Reframe:** POS covers POS; it doesn't cover SEO, review mgmt, network security, PCI scope beyond POS, or incident response.

2. **"I don't have time to implement."**
   - **Why:** Legit — he runs 8 units.
   - **Reframe:** Done-for-you SEO / review mgmt; PCI / network projects scheduled in off-peak windows.

3. **"SEO is a scam."**
   - **Why:** Burned before.
   - **Reframe:** Unlimited-hours SEO model (Services/My SEO/assets/My SEO One-Pager.html); show specific restaurant-SEO deliverables; *CHL* or hospitality case study.

4. **"I don't care about PCI."**
   - **Why:** Denial.
   - **Reframe:** Merchant bank can suspend payment processing. Class-action precedent from peer breaches. Dollar-value risk.

5. **"My MSP handles IT."**
   - **Why:** Existing vendor.
   - **Reframe:** MSP handles office; restaurants' networks / POS segmentation / SEO isn't the same animal.

6. **"How much?"**
   - **Why:** Price-first.
   - **Reframe:** Cost per new cover from SEO + risk reduction from PCI alignment. ROI anchored.

### Disqualifiers

- Single-unit café — below economics.
- Franchisee whose franchisor controls all tech — no buying power.
- Ghost-kitchen-only delivery brand — very different ICP.
