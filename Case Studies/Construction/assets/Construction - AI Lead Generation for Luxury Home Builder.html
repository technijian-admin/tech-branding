The Challenge

A boutique luxury custom home builder can’t competitively scale cold outreach. The owner’s pre-automation workflow: manually check 10 permit portals on 5 different software platforms, read monthly design-review-board agendas, monitor 60+ HOA architectural review committees, watch for story-pole installs, track coastal development permits, skim Zillow/Redfin for buyer-agent signals, follow architect/designer Instagram, and skip-trace owners.

That’s 15–20 hours a week of pattern-matching public data before saying a word to a prospect. It scales linearly with cities covered — it doesn’t scale at all in real terms.

  • 10 permit portals across 5 software platforms (Tyler EnerGov, eTRAKiT, Maintstar, custom ASP.NET)
  • 60+ HOA ARC committees across Newport Coast, Dana Point, Laguna Beach, Irvine
  • 5 coastal cities with Coastal Development Permit tracking
  • Design Review Board agendas on Granicus, Legistar, CivicPlus
  • California Gov Code §7928.205 — public records can’t disclose owner names; must skip-trace
  • Signals precede permits by 2–4 months; builder was seeing them too late
The Solution — 7-Layer Signal Architecture

A multi-agent AI system that watches the signals that precede the permit, not just the permit itself. Each layer is a discrete agent with its own portal-scraping logic, retry behavior, and output schema. The orchestrator deduplicates across layers and resolves addresses to APNs to stitch a single lead record from signals that appeared months apart.

Enrichment & Scoring

Multi-source enrichment per lead: APN via county assessor + permit, assessed value via OC Assessor API, Laserfiche link to scanned permit docs, owner name + mailing address via ATTOM (dual-path APN / address fallback), phone + email via BatchData skip-trace, architect / designer parsed from plan-set PDF title block.

Pipeline correctly handles California Gov Code §7928.205 (prohibits online disclosure of property-owner names from OC Tax Collector, Assessor, Recorder) — goes straight to paid skip-trace for names, saving cycles an analyst would waste.

What This Unlocks
  • 3–6 months of earlier detection. The builder is in the conversation before the prospect has picked a contractor — the signals that precede a permit (just-sold, ARC filings, story poles, DRB agendas) surface first.
  • No missed cities. Manual monitoring of 10 portals across 5 software platforms doesn’t happen reliably on any given week; the automation never skips a city.
  • Regulatory-aware. Knows the limits of public property data in California; routes to paid skip-trace for owner names instead of wasting runtime on sources that can’t legally disclose.
  • Compounding. Every run is indexed; a lead that appeared at Layer 1 (just-sold) 3 months ago is reconciled with Layer 5 (permit filed) today — same APN, complete story.
