The Challenge

A healthcare practice had 18 Windows 10 workstations serving front-office and clinical staff. With Windows 10 approaching end-of-support and cyber-insurance carriers tightening language around unsupported operating systems, the practice needed to move to Windows 11 — without disrupting patient scheduling, charting, or billing during business hours.

The practice wanted a weekend cutover that would upgrade the majority of devices in a single window, with a structured plan for the ~50% of machines that traditionally fail in-place upgrades and need individual attention.

  • 18 endpoints to move from Windows 10 to Windows 11
  • Cyber-insurance exposure from unsupported OS baseline
  • Business-hours sensitivity — no disruption to patient workflow
  • Realistic failure rate — in-place upgrades fail ~25-50% on first pass
  • Need for documented success for compliance and insurance reviews
  • Need weekend coverage with U.S.-based techs available to troubleshoot
The Solution

Technijian delivered the upgrade as a two-phase program: a weekend Phase 1 mass in-place upgrade via MyRemote (ScreenConnect), followed by a targeted Phase 2 that addressed every machine that failed the first pass — including swapping in preconfigured spare laptops from Technijian HQ where remediation wasn’t practical.

Phase 1 — Weekend Mass Upgrade
In-Place via MyRemote
18 eligible machines upgraded after hours via MyRemote / ScreenConnect. Upgrade success monitored in real time; detailed report generated post-upgrade. Target: 75% of devices upgraded by end of weekend. Users informed of schedule in advance.
Phase 2 — Targeted Remediation
Per-Device Fix or Swap
Every machine that failed Phase 1 assessed individually. Root cause identified (TPM, driver, storage, hardware eligibility). Fix applied where possible; otherwise, spare laptop upgraded at Technijian HQ and swapped in for the user. Daily progress updates during this phase.
Technical & Operational Highlights
  • Off-peak window — weekend rollout minimized clinical disruption
  • MyRemote automation — remote in-place upgrade at scale
  • Pre-upgrade backup on every device before attempting the upgrade
  • Spare-laptop swap path — preconfigured Windows 11 loaners at HQ
  • Real-time monitoring + daily progress reports through Phase 2 tail

The Results

The practice came out of the weekend with the majority of endpoints already on Windows 11 and a structured Phase 2 tail for the remainder. Every device was accounted for in the upgrade report. Cyber-insurance and compliance reviews could point to a dated, documented, and reconciled upgrade event rather than a loose "we got most of them done."

Representative Work
Pre-weekend staging — Inventory confirmed eligible endpoints; backups verified; user-comms checklist distributed; spare laptops pre-imaged and staged at Technijian HQ for swap-path scenarios.
Phase 1 weekend rollout — Connected to each PC after hours via MyRemote (ScreenConnect), kicked off in-place Windows 10 → Windows 11 upgrade, monitored progress, captured outcome per device.
Real-time dashboarding — Upgrade success monitored in real time via MyRemote; technicians on hand to troubleshoot devices mid-upgrade; post-upgrade reports generated for review.
Phase 2 assessment — Every Phase-1 failure reviewed individually to determine whether it was a remediable issue (driver, TPM, storage) or a hardware-eligibility problem.
Spare-laptop swaps — Preconfigured spare laptops upgraded to Windows 11 at Technijian HQ, then swapped with users' Windows 10 devices where the original hardware couldn’t cleanly upgrade.
Final reconciliation — Complete upgrade report produced: per-device status, resolution path, and final Windows 11 baseline confirmed across the fleet.
