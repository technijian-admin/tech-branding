## §05 — Regulatory / compliance environment (document-heavy, deadline-driven = AI-friendly)

- **California Political Reform Act of 1974**, administered by the **Fair Political Practices Commission
  (FPPC)**. State lobbying registration/disclosure (filed w/ Secretary of State): **Form 604** (cert),
  quarterly **Forms 615 / 625 / 635 / 645**, due last day of the month after each quarter. Campaign/PAC:
  **Form 460** (recipient committee), **461** (major donor/IE), 450, 470.
- **Local advocacy is separately regulated** — critical for an OC land-use firm: **City of LA** Municipal
  Lobbying Ordinance (Ethics Commission registration + quarterly reports); **Orange County** ordinance
  (2011; register if paid $500+/month; $75 one-time + $50 annual); **Anaheim** registry + quarterly reports;
  many OC cities have distinct rules — a **multi-jurisdiction compliance matrix**.
- **Penalties / pain:** late Form 460 = ~$10/day per filing; enforcement penalties up to **$5,000 per
  violation**. Pain = deadline-driven, multi-jurisdiction filings + disclosure accuracy + conflict tracking
  across many simultaneous clients.
- **AI constraint (the honest framing):** this is **disclosure/recordkeeping compliance, NOT automated
  decision-making** (unlike credit/lending — far less fraught). No prohibition on AI for drafting,
  deadline-tracking, or summarizing legislation. Two guardrails: (1) filings are **sworn and penalized**, so
  AI assists/QA-checks but a **human verifies and signs**; (2) lobbying disclosures are **public record** (low
  confidentiality risk in monitoring), but client *strategy* docs still need standard data hygiene.
- **MHP conversion framework:** **Gov. Code §65863.7** — before converting/closing a park, the proponent
  files a **Relocation/Conversion Impact Report (RIR)** with a relocation plan, delivered to residents ≥60
  days before the hearing, and may owe in-place market value of the mobilehome. Statutory **minimum**; locals
  can impose stricter rules. **Mobilehome Residency Law (MRL), Civil Code §798 et seq.** (90-day rent-increase
  notice). **AB 2782 (2020)** — long-term leases signed on/after 2/13/2020 no longer escape local rent
  stabilization (tightened the owner's position).
