## STRATEGIC READ (the wedge)

**This is not a managed-IT pitch. Marissa Espinoza already has IT handled.** Alexandria House runs a competent all-in-one platform (NationBuilder), an active ~15K Instagram, Givebutter event fundraising, Charity Navigator 3-star credibility, and a Candid transparency seal. They are not technologically lost.

What they are is **capacity-constrained and revenue-exposed** — and that is exactly the gap AI closes:

- **~83% of revenue is contributions/grants** (FY2024). They live and die by donor development and grant wins, not fee income.
- They posted a **~$994K single-year operating deficit in FY2024** while scaling a newly acquired 39-unit building. Every recovered staff hour and every incremental gift matters.
- They serve **women and children moving out of homelessness** in a heavily Latino/Korean neighborhood — **yet their entire website is English-only.** Their mission audience can't read their own donate or crisis-help pages in Spanish.
- A live macro risk: **HUD suspended federal homelessness funding to LAHSA in June 2026.** Diversifying and deepening private/individual giving is now urgent, not optional.

**The Technijian offer, framed for a small mission-driven org:** AI to **raise more money, win more grants, reach more families bilingually, and recover staff time** — so more of every dollar reaches the women and children they serve. AI drafts and researches; humans keep every relationship and verify every claim. Respectful, guardrailed, affordable (most of the stack qualifies for free/granted nonprofit tech).

## HONEST AI GUARDRAILS
*Non-negotiables — especially for an org serving vulnerable women and children. Bundle a one-page AI use policy + light staff training as a deliverable (76% of nonprofits have no policy; 69% of AI-using marketers have no training — 2025).*

1. **Hallucination control — every funder claim must be true.** AI generates *a* response, not necessarily an *accurate* one. **Rule: AI drafts; a human verifies every number, name, citation, and outcome claim before submission.** One fabricated stat in a funder report is relationship-ending and potentially contract-breaching. *(NonProfitPRO, BoardEffect, 2025)*
2. **Donor & beneficiary PII + dignity — never feed client data to public AI.** Data on women and children escaping unsafe housing carries legal/contractual privacy duties; donor confidentiality is often an award condition. **Rule: no client/beneficiary names, locations, or case details into consumer LLMs; use enterprise tooling with no-training terms (e.g., M365 Copilot tenant) and de-identified data.** AI must never re-expose or profile survivors. *(Perlman & Perlman; ASU Lodestar, 2026)*
3. **The human owns the relationship.** **Rule: AI for prospect research, segmentation, and first-draft copy; humans own every major-gift conversation and final donor message.** *(Nonprofit Productivity Report, 2025)*
4. **Funder AI-disclosure — disclose, and follow each funder's rule.** Policies vary: **NIH** (eff. Sept 25, 2025) bars fully AI-generated applications and AI-fabricated references; **Spencer Foundation** requires disclosing generative-AI use; **NSF** encourages indicating use; **Wellcome** requires declaration except language help. **Rule: check each funder's AI policy before applying; keep AI to assistive drafting; disclose when required.** *(Thesify, Spencer Foundation, 2025)*
5. **Bilingual quality gate.** Machine translation alone misses cultural/idiomatic nuance — a native bilingual reviewer validates any community-distributed Spanish material before publish.

## 1. What they do (mission & model)

Alexandria House is a **transitional residence and "house of hospitality"** providing safe, supportive housing for **women and children experiencing homelessness and trauma**, helping them move from emergency shelter to economic/emotional stability and, ultimately, permanent housing. It also operates as a **neighborhood/community center** serving the surrounding Mid-Wilshire / Koreatown community, and engages in **grassroots advocacy** against systemic poverty. ([alexandriahouse.org home](https://www.alexandriahouse.org/), [About](https://www.alexandriahouse.org/about), [Angelus News](https://angelusnews.com/local/la-catholics/sister-judy-vaughn-alexandria-house/))

- Self-description: **"intentionally multicultural and anti-racist,"** centered on **"the needs of women who are economically poor."** Motto/values shorthand on the About page: **"HOPE. LOVE. HEALING."** ([About](https://www.alexandriahouse.org/about))
- Service model = **"Circle of Support"** that strengthens **six essential areas of stability: (1) housing, (2) economic opportunity, (3) food security, (4) child care, (5) social-emotional wellness, (6) lifelong alumni support.** ([Home](https://www.alexandriahouse.org/))
- Original **three-part founding vision**: (1) transitional housing bridging emergency shelter to permanent housing, (2) a vibrant neighborhood center, (3) grassroots advocacy against systemic poverty. ([Angelus News](https://angelusnews.com/local/la-catholics/sister-judy-vaughn-alexandria-house/))

# Research Brief D — Nonprofit-AI Landscape for a Small/Mid Social-Services Nonprofit (Transitional Housing for Women & Children)

**Prepared for:** Technijian AI Growth Blueprint | **Date:** 2026-06-16
**Scope:** How AI helps a budget-constrained social-services nonprofit grow funding & impact, sector adoption/funding-gap stats, low-cost tech-access programs, and honest nonprofit-specific guardrails. Every statistic is labeled with source + year; unsourced items are flagged.

---

## 0. The structural problem AI is being hired to solve (context for the whole blueprint)

A small transitional-housing nonprofit lives the classic capacity squeeze:

- **Grants are a hard, slow, low-yield channel for small orgs.** 86% of nonprofits pursue government/foundation grants, but **nearly two-thirds secure only $5,000–$15,000/year** and receive just **2–4 grants/year**; the average wait before a **first** grant is **2 years, 4 months**, and **41% of nonprofits lack the readiness** to apply for, receive, and manage grant funding (BryteBridge "Growing Pains" report, 2024, n=261).
- **Grant writing is enormously time-expensive.** A single federal grant application takes an average of **80–200 hours** (SSIR / Blue Avocado grant-writing-infrastructure pieces — see Sources; year not individually dated, flag below).
- **Small orgs rarely have dedicated development staff.** Development/grant duties are typically absorbed by the ED and program leads on top of their day jobs; **development-staff turnover averages ~16–18 months** where grant writing is housed (SSIR, *Grant Writing Infrastructure*; specific year not stated — **FLAG**).
- **Grants are the minority of revenue anyway.** Individual donors drive **54% of nonprofit revenue** vs. grants at **~22%** (foundation + government combined) (BryteBridge, 2024) — so AI value is highest when it strengthens **both** grant capacity **and** individual-donor development.

**Implication for the blueprint:** AI's pitch to this client is *time recovery + reach*, not "replace your fundraiser." Each admin hour AI removes is an hour redirected to the mission and to the human relationships that actually close gifts.

---

## 1. How AI concretely helps a small nonprofit GROW funding & impact

### (1) Grant prospecting & drafting
- **Prospecting:** Candid's **Foundation Directory** (the established funder-research database) is merging GuideStar + Foundation Directory data with machine learning to move from search to "strategic intelligence" (which funders match, not just that they exist). **Instrumentl** is the leading discovery platform and now flags *gaps between a funder's stated guidelines and its actual giving patterns* via its Prospecting Assistant. (Instrumentl blog, 2025; One Hundred Nights, 2025.)
- **LLM-assisted first drafts with human edit:** **Instrumentl Apply** is an integrated AI drafting module trained on its proprietary 990s/funder-insight database that also learns from your past proposals to match your voice; **Grantable** lets you upload materials, co-edit with AI, and generate funder-aligned drafts. (Instrumentl, 2025; FreeWill / Grant Assistant tool roundups, 2026.)
- **Adoption signal:** ~**25% of nonprofits already use AI for grant-writing assistance** (TechSoup × Tapp Network, *2025 AI Benchmark Report*, Feb 2025).
- **Mission framing:** AI drafts the boilerplate (org background, need statement, standard outcomes language) so the ED/program lead spend their limited hours on the funder-specific argument and the numbers — turning an 80–200-hour federal application into a far smaller human-edit lift.

### (2) Donor development — segmentation, major-gift prospect research, personalized appeals, lapsed-donor re-engagement
- **Predictive segmentation & scoring:** Tools like **Dataro** use propensity modeling to predict who is likely to give one-time, recurring, mid-level, major, or planned gifts; **Hatch** offers explainable affluence/propensity/affinity/RFM scoring. (Dataro, 2025; Hatch, 2026.)

## 4. HONEST GUARDRAILS specific to nonprofits (non-negotiables for this client)

1. **Hallucination in grant narratives — every funder claim must be true.** "AI models are trained to generate *a* response, not necessarily an *accurate* one" (hallucinations). AI-drafted reports can also overstate favorable data and bury complex/long-term outcomes, misaligning with mission. **Rule: AI drafts, a human verifies every number, name, citation, and outcome claim before submission.** (NonProfitPRO; BoardEffect, 2025.) For a small org, one fabricated statistic in a funder report is a relationship-ending and potentially contract-breaching event.

2. **Donor & beneficiary PII + dignity — never feed client data to public AI.** Beneficiary data (especially for a vulnerable population — women and children escaping unstable/unsafe housing) is frequently subject to legal/contractual privacy obligations; donor/grantor confidentiality is often a condition of the award. Disclosure can breach contracts or privacy law. **Rule: no client/beneficiary names, locations, or case details into public/consumer LLMs; use enterprise tooling with no-training data terms (e.g., M365 Copilot tenant) and de-identified data for any AI workflow.** (Perlman & Perlman; ASU Lodestar, 2026.) Add explicit vulnerable-population sensitivity: AI must not be used in ways that re-expose or profile survivors.

3. **The human relationship in major gifts — AI assists, never replaces.** Data backs the instinct: 82% comfortable with AI for *research*, 63% wary of AI for *communications* (personalization concern). **Rule: AI for prospect research, segmentation, and first-draft copy; humans own every major-gift conversation and final donor message.** (Nonprofit Productivity Report, 2025.)

4. **Funder AI-disclosure norms — disclose, and follow each funder's rule.** Policies now vary by funder: **NIH** (effective Sept 25, 2025 receipt date) prohibits presenting AI-generated data/references as real and prohibits *fully* AI-generated applications; **Spencer Foundation** requires disclosing generative-AI use in LOIs, proposals, and progress reports (assistive AI exempt); **NSF** encourages indicating whether/how genAI was used; **Wellcome** requires declaration except for language help. **Rule: check each funder's AI policy before applying, keep AI use to assistive drafting, and disclose when required.** (Thesify; Spencer Foundation; thesciencetalk, 2025.)

5. **Governance reality check.** 76% of nonprofits have no AI policy and 69% of AI-using marketers have no training (2025) — so the blueprint should **bundle a one-page AI use policy + light staff training** as a deliverable, not assume it exists. This is also increasingly a board/fiduciary expectation. (State of AI in Nonprofits / BoardSource-adjacent guidance via BoardEffect, 2025.)
