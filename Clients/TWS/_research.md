**Implication:** Growth engine framing = account intelligence (identify districts with new wellness funding or new leadership triggers) + thought leadership SEO (districts Google "trauma-informed professional development California") + outcome documentation. ## 08 — Market Context

### California Funding Window (2026)
- **$8B combined investment:** CYBHI (Children and Youth Behavioral Health Initiative) + CCSPP (California Community Schools Partnership Program)
- **FY2026:** $1.7B discretionary block grant to districts based on ADA; $2.1B LCFF COLA increase
- **Federal cuts:** Trump administration cancelling $168M in school mental health grants — creating urgency for state-funded solutions
- **Implication:** California districts have a rare multi-year funding window NOW for mental health/wellness initiatives

### Teacher Burnout Crisis (2025-2026)
- 53% of K-12 teachers report burnout (RAND 2025, down from 60% in 2024 but still crisis)
- Top driver: student behavior management (52%)
- 78% cite insufficient administrative support
- Higher rates: Female teachers (60%), Teachers of Color (58-59%)
- ThriveWell's Staff Resilience + Improving Staff Culture programs directly address this

### Market Size
- CA school district mental health spending: $934M (2018-19) → $1.64B (2023-24)
- 37% of public schools still lack formal SEL curriculum (implementation gap = opportunity)
- ~1,000+ California school districts; even 50-100 at $60K average = $3-6M ARR

### Restorative Justice Legislative Tailwinds
- Restorative Practices in Schools Act gaining traction
- Senate bills (Bennet, Cohen) promoting RJ as discipline alternative
- NIJ investing in restorative justice research
- CA increasingly mandating trauma-informed approaches
