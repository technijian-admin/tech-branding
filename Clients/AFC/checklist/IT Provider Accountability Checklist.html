The IT Provider Accountability Checklist

Ten questions you should be able to ask any IT provider — your current one or your next one — and get a clear, documented answer. If a question gets a vague answer twice, you’ve found the gap.

Closure & Communication

1. Who confirms a ticket is done — you, or us? Good: a ticket closes only when the person who raised it confirms the fix.
2. Can we see every open ticket, its owner, and its status in plain English? Good: a live list you can read without translation.
3. Who is on our account — a named team, or whoever picks up? Good: the same small pod every time, so staff never re-explain history.
4. What do we receive monthly that a non-technical leader can read? Good: a plain-language report — what happened, what it means, what’s next.

Root Cause & Recommendations

5. When the same issue comes back, what changes? Good: a recurring issue triggers a root-cause review — not a third quick fix.
6. Are recommendations written down — problem, fix, investment, owner, date? Good: a tracked list, reviewed until done. Advice shouldn’t evaporate.
7. What evidence shows our machines are patched, backed up, and protected? Good: documented proof on a schedule — not “trust us.”

Planning Ahead

8. When do we sit down with leadership to review the whole picture? Good: a standing quarterly review — without you having to ask for it.
9. If we move offices, who owns the IT side — and when do circuits get ordered? Good: a written plan scheduled backward from move day; circuits first (30–60+ days lead).
10. Are we right-licensed — including nonprofit pricing where we qualify? Good: an annual licensing review that finds savings before you ask.
