Three motions, ABM-framed: **Get Found & Trusted** (authority) → **Win the Work** (project/permit intelligence + bid response) → **Run Leaner & Remember** (estimating acceleration + knowledge).

### C. THE STRATEGIC THESIS — rebalance toward the booming side, win more bids with fewer estimators.
**Two diverging demand engines.** PUI's **residential developer/builder** channel is cooling across CA/NV/AZ in 2026 (starts down, deliveries pulling back), while its **grid / public-infrastructure** channel is in a once-in-a-generation boom (~$1.4T US utility capex 2025–2030; ~$178B transmission 2025–2028; 8–10% distribution growth; IIJA grid undergrounding + EV charging + broadband + streetlights/signals). PUI is positioned for the boom — it already does **high-voltage, Rule 20 undergrounding, and streetlights/traffic signals**, and hired David Pettersen (ex-SDG&E / Butsko-NV5) to lead the municipal/utility focus. Meanwhile the **skilled-labor shortage** (electricians retiring faster than replaced) makes **estimating bandwidth and crew productivity the binding constraint**. So the AI thesis writes itself: **use AI to chase and win more of the booming grid/public work — faster, more accurate bids + project/permit intelligence — without adding estimators PUI can't hire.**

### D. THE MOAT / boundary — AI assists, the licensed estimator/PM signs the bid.
PUI's work is **fixed-price and safety-critical** (high-voltage is life-safety; "there is no such thing as discount high voltage"). On a hard bid, a 15–20% estimate error transfers risk to the contractor's PL insurer, which may not cover AI-generated figures not validated by a qualified estimator. So the boundary is the credibility line: **AI accelerates takeoffs, bid assembly, and project intelligence; the licensed estimator / PM owns final pricing and signs off.** Same "AI assists, credentialed human signs" boundary as [[project_acu]] / [[project_vaf]] — state it plainly; it builds trust.

### E. The distinctive differentiators (the spine).
1. **One contractor, all five trades** — wet + dry + high-voltage + streetlights/traffic-signals + utility engineering under one roof. Rivals are siloed (Doty = dry; T.E. Roberts/Excel = wet; Sturgeon/Helix = electrical). The unowned story: **single-source all underground scopes → de-risk the developer's schedule.**
2. **"Applicant Installation"** — Daniel Mole pioneered the model letting developers self-install power infrastructure for schedule + cost control. PUI's origin differentiator; almost unmarketed online.
3. **Employee-owned (ESOP since 2017)** — a culture/retention asset in a labor-short market; ties to the "craftspeople" brand.
4. **Tri-state footprint (CA + NV + AZ)** — a structural AEO advantage: own answer-visibility in three states where most rivals are single-state.

## 03 — Industry & Market Dynamics (2025–2026, sourced)

**Two diverging engines (the core strategic story):**
- **Residential (cooling):** CA/NV/AZ housing starts down, deliveries pulling back in 2026; SoCal metros posted YoY price declines; Phoenix MPCs ~half of new-home sales with a deep long-run pipeline but a 2026 delivery pullback; Las Vegas builders logged the lowest February net sales in a decade (−22% YoY). PUI's developer/builder channel softens near-term.
- **Grid / public infrastructure (booming):** ~**$1.4T** US regulated-utility capex 2025–2030 (≈ double the prior decade); ~**$178B** transmission construction 2025–2028 (largest cycle ever); distribution +8–10%; 2023 utilities spent **$11.8B on underground power lines** + **$7.5B on transformers** (+23% YoY). Federal: **IIJA ~$73B** grid modernization incl. undergrounding; **DOE GRIP $10.5B** + a $5B hardening program (eligible: undergrounding); **NEVI $5B** EV charging (restored Aug 2025); **BEAD $42B** broadband (construction ramps 2026). **CPUC Rule 20** undergrounding shifting from legacy 20A (work credits ended 2022, expire 2033) to **utility wildfire-mitigation undergrounding (20D / IOU hardening)** — the growing channel. **CA SB 1 (~$5B/yr)** + Caltrans transit-tech + municipal LED/connected-streetlight conversions feed the streetlights/signals line.

**Labor shortage = the "why now":** construction needs ~439K (2025) → ~499K (2026) net new workers; electricians ~7K join vs ~10K retire/yr, workforce projected −14% by 2030 while demand rises ~25%. Estimating bandwidth + license-gated crews are the bottleneck. (ABC, ECMag/BLS, AGC.)

**How contractors win work:** competitive hard-bid to GCs/developers; government public-works **low-bid RFPs**; **prequalification** (CA PCC §20101 / DIR); on-call/master agreements; relationships. Public projects avg **4.7 bids** vs 2.3 private. Speed matters (a cited stat: 85% of winning bids submitted within 72 hrs of posting). Preconstruction pain: manual takeoffs, material price/lead-time volatility, scope-gap risk on fixed-price bids; "18% of project time lost searching for data, 28% on rework" (Procore).

## 04 — AI in Construction & Specialty Contracting (sourced) + the boundary

**Adoption is real:** AGC 2026 Outlook — **61% of firms use AI or plan to increase AI investment** (up from 44%); top uses: office 45%, **estimating 23%**, design/preconstruction 20%. Construction-tech investment $3.55B in Q1 2025 (46% to AI).

**Best-fit plays for PUI:**
- **Estimating & quantity takeoff** — automated takeoff cuts preconstruction time dramatically (manual 40–60 hrs → ~1–2 hrs + human review); the #2 AI use in construction. Relieves the estimating-bandwidth bottleneck.
- **Project / permit / entitlement intelligence** — AI platforms (Mercator.ai, Dodge, ConstructConnect, BuildingConnected) mine permits, rezoning/entitlement filings, and bid boards to flag projects *months early* and match contractors to scope. **The clearest "AI as business development" play** for a developer/agency-facing contractor — know who's entitling/permitting underground+electrical scope across CA/NV/AZ first.
- **Bid/proposal generation, RFI/submittal handling, scheduling, document management, safety, digital marketing/AEO.**

**The boundary (OFF-LIMITS):** autonomous AI bidding on fixed-price, safety-critical work. A 15–20% estimate error from un-reviewed AI transfers risk to PL insurance that may not cover it. → **AI assists; the licensed estimator/PM owns final pricing and signs off.** Same boundary moat as ACU/VAF.
