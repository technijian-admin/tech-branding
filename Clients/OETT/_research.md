## THE THESIS (one line)
OETT trains the people who build California's roads, dams, tunnels, and high-rises — but the trades are hitting a retirement cliff (**~41% of the construction workforce projected to retire by 2031**, NCCER) at the same time OETT's own veteran master operators and instructors age out, taking decades of irreplaceable, hard-won know-how with them. AI does the two things a training trust exists to do, faster: (1) **capture and scale that expertise into modern curriculum**, and (2) **recruit and prepare the next generation** of operating engineers. This is mission capacity — not IT.

## 1. What OETT is (VERIFIED — oett.net, Ballotpedia, oefi.org)
- **Mission:** A non-profit trust created by **IUOE Local 12 and its signatory contractors** to fund and deliver training for both **apprentices and journeypersons**. Trust monies buy training equipment, hire instructors, and provide educational resources. Established **1964**.
- **Scale:** Between **1964–2016 the training fund trained more than 6,000 apprentices** (Ballotpedia — cite as "since 1964, as of 2016"). Local 12 is **"one of the largest and most influential locals in the IUOE"** (cite as the soft, verified scale claim; exact current membership/enrollment = discovery question).
- **Footprint:** Home office **2190 S. Pellissier Place, Whittier, CA 90601** — (562) 695-0611, office@oett.net. **Six training facilities** across Southern California + Southern Nevada: **Whittier, Devore, Rincon, Bakersfield, Camarillo, CSLO (San Luis Obispo area)**. Six apprentice **districts** (D1 LA, D2 Ventura/SB/SLO, D3 Kern, D4 San Diego, D5 Riverside/SB/Imperial, D7 Orange County). (Southern Nevada runs a sister JATC — SNOE JATC.)
- **What an Operating Engineer does (their words):** operate heavy construction equipment on high-rises, roads & freeways, residential developments, dams, waterways, airports, underground tunnels. Equipment families: **cranes, concrete pumps, pile drivers, drilling equipment, grade checking, equipment repair/maintenance, sand/gravel/asphalt/concrete plants, dredging.**
- **Programs & courses (VERIFIED):** the **Apprenticeship Program** (entry pipeline); **journeyperson continuing education**; **Inspection CEU**; **Crane Operations** (Slab & Erection); **Pipeline Training** (via the IUOE National Pipeline program); **Hazmat**; **Remote-Control Equipment**; **Drone Training**; the **Skilled & Trained Program**. In-person only; site hours **7:00 am–3:30 pm Tue–Sat**.
- **Apprenticeship structure (VERIFIED — FAQ):** work-study model = **6,000 hours OJT + 6 semesters of classroom**, typically **3–4 years**. **No cost** for the training itself; apprentices join Local 12 (~$800 initiation, ~$288/yr dues). Entry: **17½ to apply, 18 to enter; pass an entrance exam ≥70%; no prior experience required; applications open ~every 2 years** (currently **closed**). Apprentices start at **60% of journeyperson wage**, stepping up as OJT + classroom milestones are met; medical/dental, holiday/vacation, pension on qualification.
- **Existing tech (VERIFIED — site):** online **application portal**, separate **apprentice accounts** and **journeyperson accounts**, email/mail exam notifications, class registration required **14 days** before start. *(They already run member-facing systems — so the gap is the AI/curriculum/recruitment layer ON TOP, not "they have no tech." ## 3. Industry context — the retirement cliff (VERIFIED w/ sources)
- **349,000** net new construction workers needed in **2026**, rising to **~456,000** in 2027 (Associated Builders & Contractors); AGC cites **~499,000** for 2026. **More than half** of the 2026 need is just to **replace retirees**. (ABC; AGC; ConstructConnect.)
- **Aging workforce:** average US construction worker **42.5** years old; only **16% under 35**; **~40% over 45**. **NCCER projects ~41% of the current construction workforce will retire by 2031.** Apprenticeships take years to produce a fully skilled worker → the gap widens faster than it fills. (NCCER; ABC Carolinas.)
- **Institutional-memory loss:** when veteran supers/foremen/operators retire, decades of judgment and productivity leave with them — the exact problem a training trust is positioned to solve, *if* it can capture that knowledge before it walks. (Construction Dive; CIC.)
- **Why it matters to OETT:** this is the macro tailwind that makes BOTH motions urgent — recruit faster (fill the pipeline) AND capture/scale instructor knowledge (retain the expertise). Frame as opportunity + urgency, dated to the 2026→2031 window.

## 4. AI peer landscape (VERIFIED w/ sources) — "How peer training orgs adopt AI"
- **Federal tailwind (dated, powerful for the urgency callout):** the **US DOL / ETA launched a national initiative (April 2026)** + a public website to **integrate AI skills into Registered Apprenticeships nationwide** and modernize apprenticeship programs — including integrating AI competencies into **traditional trades and infrastructure occupations.** (DOL ETA releases, 2026-04-01 / 2026-04-29.)
- **NC State AI Academy + Apprenti:** first nationwide **Registered AI Associate apprenticeship** with national standards (Sept 2025).
- **AI simulators in the trades:** an **AI-guided XR welding trainer improved welding accuracy and learning rate** over traditional VR; an Indonesian **"AI teaching factory"** boosted technical proficiency and industry readiness — directly analogous to heavy-equipment sim training before live seat time.
- **GenAI for instructors:** documented frameworks for VET teachers using GenAI to **generate teaching materials and lesson plans** (peer-reviewed). (Preprints.org; MDPI; HKSMP; CSET Georgetown "State of AI-Related Apprenticeships.")
- **Framing:** these are **representative directions of travel across comparable training organizations, not guarantees** (Gate 1b). They prove the category is moving — OETT can lead its peer set in SoCal rather than follow.

## 5. Regulatory / compliance — the admin burden (VERIFIED) → an efficiency pillar
- **California DAS** (Division of Apprenticeship Standards, within DIR) administers apprenticeship law: enforces standards for wages, hours, working conditions, and the skills required for journeyperson certification. Programs/contractors generate a paper/EDI audit trail (**DAS 140 / DAS 142**, certified payroll). **Federal DOL = RAPIDS** (Registered Apprenticeship Partners Information Data System). California is moving to **electronic reporting** (online platform / EDI; the **CORD** platform for training-fund applications & reimbursement).
- **Why it matters:** registered-apprenticeship programs must stay **audit-ready and funding-eligible** — real, recurring documentation/reporting labor. AI can pre-fill, validate, and reconcile reporting (records → audit-ready) and reduce the staff hours and audit risk. (DIR/DAS; GoSprout 2026 compliance guide; apprenticeship.gov.) *Label all efficiency numbers "conservative, confirm at discovery."*
