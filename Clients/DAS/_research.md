(This is the exact error that lost the RKE engagement — see skill Phase 0.2.)
3. **Two fronts, name them honestly — GROWTH + INTEGRATION.** DAS's bigger near-term win is arguably *internal integration* (weaving AI into how a 57-year-old, EOS-run design studio actually runs — pursuits, knowledge, production, entitlement research) as much as *growth* (winning more developer pursuits). The honest boundary: AI removes the non-design drag (proposal assembly, precedent search, entitlement/code research, knowledge retrieval, marketing production) so licensed architects spend more time designing and in front of clients.
5. **Do not invent.** No fabricated project names, client names, award counts beyond what the site states (775+ awards, 6,353 projects, 1M+ units, 44 states, 15 countries, founded 1968/57 yrs), or revenue/headcount precision. Headcount is an estimate (~15+ senior/leadership shown; full firm likely ~40–70 across 3 offices — label as estimate). Don't cite a revenue number — none reliable.

## 05 — The Market & Where It's Going (2025–2026) — grounds the "why now"

**A. California housing demand is being legislated UP — directly into DAS's exact product lines.**
- **SB 79** (signed Oct 10, 2025; effective **Jul 1, 2026**) — upzones around transit, including small single-family parcels → more **multifamily + mixed-use** near transit.
- **SB 1211** — up to **8 detached ADUs** per multifamily lot → ADU volume on DAS's multifamily projects.
- **SB 543 / AB 1061** — ADU processing streamlining + SB 9 expansion → more **middle-housing / lot-split** design work.
- 60,000+ ADU permits in CA since 2018; sustained state pressure to produce housing.
- **Implication:** the regulatory wind is at DAS's back specifically in **multifamily, BTR, ADU, and middle housing** — the firm that can *research entitlement pathways and turn pursuits around fastest* captures disproportionate share. That speed is an AI play.

**B. AEC is at an AI inflection — and most firms are NOT ready (the opening).**
- **53%** of A&E firms now use AI tools (2025 Deltek Clarity); **94%** of users will increase usage in 2026.
- **75%** of AEC firms expect AI to boost profitability, but only **~20%** feel "highly prepared" — a **55-point readiness gap**.
- ~**40%** of design-development tasks are automatable; document analysis (specs/RFIs/RFPs), precedent/code search, and early massing/site-plan iteration are the proven near-term use cases.
- "The biggest barriers in 2026 aren't cost — they're complexity, culture, and connection. Success requires not just tools but training and an integrated approach." → **This is the gap a fractional AI partner fills.** DAS doesn't need to hire an AI department; it needs an integration partner who brings the strategy, the build, and the governance.
- The gap between AI-adopting and AI-resistant firms is becoming "a measurable competitive disadvantage."

**The "why now" in one line:** demand for DAS's exact products is being legislated upward in 2026 while the AEC field is split into AI-ready and AI-behind firms — DAS can lock in the ready side, against rivals who are larger but not necessarily faster.
