## 00 — Positioning Guardrail (READ FIRST)

### A. SC Fuels is a Pilot Company (Berkshire Hathaway) subsidiary — NOT a scrappy family independent.

The single biggest accuracy risk in this engagement: the **"third-generation family-owned"** language still on scfuels.com is **stale legacy copy**. SC Fuels was acquired by **Pilot Company (Pilot Travel Centers LLC)** — close **November 1, 2021**, announced December 2021 — and Pilot is majority-owned by **Berkshire Hathaway**. SC Fuels today is the **West Coast commercial-distribution arm of a top-tier North American fuel company**, with Pilot's balance sheet, supply infrastructure, and the CFN/cardlock network behind it. The Greinke leadership stayed on to run the business unit.

SC Fuels is also **the consolidator/acquirer** in the West: it acquired **Downs Energy** cardlock/fueling/lubricants assets (50+ cardlock sites, closed Oct 1, 2025) and **Lutz Petroleum** (Dec 2025).

**Frame the blueprint as:** help the **market leader defend share, integrate acquisitions, and out-modernize the digital-native disruptors (Booster)** — NOT "help the local underdog survive." Pitching them as small would be a credibility error.

### B. GTM motion = HYBRID, leaning ACCOUNT-BASED (like ORX, not like a consumer service business).

SC Fuels wins primarily through a **regional B2B field-sales force calling on named commercial/fleet accounts** (regional, product-specific sales hotlines with direct extensions; consultative "let us design a solution" CTAs; Fortune-500-scale negotiated supply contracts). This is account-based.

BUT — exactly like ORX — there **is** genuine searchable demand: people google "bulk diesel delivery [city]," "cardlock fuel card near me," "DEF delivery," "renewable diesel supplier [region]." So the program is honestly **hybrid**:
- **Searchable demand** → AEO/SEO + local/location pages + on-site capture (honest; the demand exists and SC Fuels under-harvests it).
- **Named high-value accounts** (large fleets, construction/ag operations, municipal RFPs) → account intelligence + per-account outreach + RFP automation under the existing field-sales strategy.

**Do NOT** lead with "broad lead generation," "fill your funnel," or "shotgun marketing." See `feedback_pitch_abm_not_leadgen.md`. AI **supports** the rep relationship and Pilot's supply scale — it does not replace them. State that boundary plainly; it is a trust signal.

### C. Numbers discipline (client-facing accuracy).

- **Do NOT cite a hard revenue figure.** Data-broker estimates conflict wildly ($394M vs $650M) and are unreliable for a private Pilot subsidiary (revenue consolidates into Pilot). Use **gallon volume** (with a caveat) and verified anchors instead.
- **Do NOT print unverified leadership names** (President/VP names are data-broker-sourced and may be stale after 4+ years under Pilot). Only **Steven P. Greinke (CEO)** is primary-source-verified. Keep other names out of the client doc; list "confirm sponsor/leadership" as a discovery question.
- Every projection is **estimated/conservative** pending discovery (see §11).

## 04 — Industry Context, Regulation & Trends

### Trends 2024–2026 (verified)
- **Heavy consolidation** — 30 fuel-distribution/c-store deals in 2024; refiners (Phillips 66, Pilot, World Kinect) buying distributors to lock in branded throughput. **SC Fuels is on the buy side** (Downs, Lutz).
- **Margin pressure + energy transition** — US gasoline consumption fell in 2025 even as miles rose (efficiency); EV share of new heavy-truck sales ~22% H1 2025. Depot-based medium-duty fleet electrification is the direct encroachment on commercial-fleet fuel demand.
- **Renewable fuels = the growth/margin offset** — renewable diesel (R99) is a drop-in substitute; LCFS credits drive the economics. SC Fuels already sells it — room to lead.
- **Telematics/fleet-management convergence** — fuel cards + telematics + tank sensors merging; ~14% of fleet fuel spend lost to fraud/theft; AI route/idle optimization claims 10–20% fleet savings.
- **Driver/labor shortage** (CDL/Hazmat) pressures delivery ops.

### Regulatory burden (include a Regulatory section — this is a regulated client, and the burden is the AI thesis)
| Regime | What it requires | AI-reduction |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| **CARB / LCFS** | Carbon-intensity tracking; credit/deficit accounting; quarterly reporting. CI target stepped to **22.75% July 1, 2025**. | **High** — automate CI/credit tracking + report generation |
| **RFS / RINs** (EPA) | Renewable Identification Number tracking, RVO, attestation | **High** — RIN position tracking + audit-ready records |
| **IFTA** (fuel tax) | Multi-jurisdiction mileage/fuel tax filing (~88 hrs/yr, ~$4,400/fleet manual) | **High** — auto data capture + ready-to-file returns |
| **UST rules** (CA SWRCB/EPA) | Annual monitoring cert; 36-mo tank testing; **single-wall tanks closed by Dec 31, 2025** | **Medium** — compliance-calendar + doc automation |
| **DOT / Hazmat** | Driver/Hazmat certs, BOL/manifest, placarding | **Medium** — doc + cert-expiry automation |
| **Weights & Measures** | Meter accuracy/certification (confirm county program before citing specifics) | Low–Medium |
| **Fuel excise/sales tax** | Federal + state + local excise layering | Medium |

LCFS + RIN + IFTA are the three highest-ROI compliance-automation targets — heavy, recurring, error-prone, and CARB/LCFS is *uniquely Western*, so SC Fuels' footprint makes it especially acute.

### Gap analysis — white space SC Fuels can own
1. **AI/content/AEO narrative in the West.** SC Fuels is **AI-blank and content-light**. Mansfield & World Kinect lead on content/data; Booster leads on UX + named AI. SC Fuels already beats the dated regionals (Hunt & Sons, Ramos) and has a real self-serve portal — but nobody in its Western backyard has claimed the AI/answer-engine/compliance-automation story. First mover wins compounding inbound.
2. **Local search demand is under-harvested.** ~15 branches, **no per-city/per-branch location landing pages** despite real "[fuel service] + [city]" demand.
3. **The Booster experience bar.** A tech-forward fuel buyer now expects an app, transparency, and a decarbonization story. SC Fuels can adopt the good UX (portal + chatbot + data) without the startup burn — backed by Pilot's supply.
4. **Acquisition integration as a growth lever.** SC Fuels is buying companies (Downs, Lutz). AI account-intelligence + a knowledge graph turns each acquired customer book into cross-sell and clean onboarding, not churn.
5. **The one structural disadvantage:** SC Fuels lacks the **MBE/WBE diversity-spend status** that Pinnacle (WBE) and Ramos (MBE) use to win municipal set-aside RFPs. Counter with **bid-response speed (RFP automation) + Pilot scale + service depth** — not a diversity credential it doesn't have.
