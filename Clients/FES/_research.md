## THE THESIS (one line)
Franklin Educational Services is built on one person's rare expertise - Dr. Daniel Franklin's attachment-based "Academic Management" method - delivered by a vetted team to families of kids with learning differences. AI's two highest-value roles here are (1) **giving the clinical team their time back** from the heavy documentation that educational therapy requires, and (2) **turning Daniel's authority into the way more of the right families find the practice** - all on a foundation of strict privacy for children's data.

## GTM / FRAMING
- **Primary motion = broad demand-gen + authority + referral nurture** (NOT ABM). Affluent LA/OC parents of struggling kids find help by (a) **searching** ("dyslexia tutor Los Angeles," "educational therapist Brentwood," "ADHD executive-function coach teen"), (b) **professional referrals** (schools, neuropsychologists, pediatricians, speech/OT therapists), and (c) **Daniel's authority** (his book, 80+ talks, media). The growth engine serves all three.
- **Two fronts, weighted toward efficiency + trust** given the clinical/child context: GROW (be found, authority content, referral nurture, capture inquiries) and RUN (documentation automation, methodology capture, tutor-student matching, parent communication).
- **Data privacy is the foundation, not a footnote.** This practice handles children's learning, behavioral, and diagnosis-adjacent data (dyslexia/ADHD/autism/processing). Responsible-AI must lead: never put a child's records into public AI tools; private, governed deployments; human-in-the-loop on anything reaching a parent or child. FERPA-adjacent + clinical confidentiality. This is the trust differentiator (their brand is compassion + trust) and ties to Ravi's CISSP / security-first method.
- **Founder-dependency is the strategic risk + opportunity.** Daniel IS the brand and the method. Capturing his "Academic Management" methodology into training/playbooks (so a vetted team delivers it consistently and onboards faster) scales his impact - frame respectfully as amplifying him, never replacing him.

## 2. Stakeholder personas (build ~5)
1. **The Parent (the buyer)** - anxious, often affluent Westside parent of a struggling child; wants trust, expertise, and visible progress. AI: be findable; a private intake assistant; transparent progress updates. *(Core Orange)*
2. **The Student (K-12, learning differences)** - needs personalized, confidence-building support and executive-function scaffolding. AI: adaptive practice and study tools, human-guided; never autonomous for a child. *(Core Blue)*
3. **The Educational Therapist / Tutor (staff)** - expert practitioners losing hours to session notes, progress reports, and assessment write-ups. AI: draft documentation (human-reviewed) so they teach more and write less. *(Teal)*
4. **The Referral Partner (school, neuropsychologist, pediatrician, SLP/OT)** - sends families, needs reliable communication and outcomes. AI: referral nurture, professional outcome summaries. *(Dark Charcoal)*
5. **Dr. Daniel Franklin / Clinical Director (founder)** - the methodology and the brand; the key-person risk. AI: capture the "Academic Management" method into training and playbooks so the team delivers it consistently; surface his authority; free his time. *(Critical Red - the key-person persona)*

## 3. Field context - AI in tutoring & special education (VERIFIED w/ sources; about the FIELD, not FES)
- **Documentation is the time sink AI most clearly relieves.** Special-education teams report **saving ~2-4 hours per IEP** using AI for initial drafts (goals, progress-note templates, accommodation lists, parent-communication drafts); **~57%** of special-ed teachers find AI helpful for recommendations and spotting learning patterns. (EdTech Magazine; Council for Exceptional Children; Structural Learning - 2026.)
- **Intelligent tutoring + adaptive learning** (e.g., Khanmigo) personalize pace and target struggle points; 2026 consensus "best uses" = personalized tutoring, AI-supported assessment/feedback, and offloading routine workload. (ETC Journal; Faculty Focus - 2026.)
- **Parent communication:** AI-generated progress summaries strengthen the home-school connection - with the explicit caveat that **technology complements rather than replaces the human touch** (exactly Daniel's attachment-based philosophy). 
- **Frame all peer examples as "representative directions of travel across comparable practices, not guarantees."**
