## 3. How they win work (the bid cycle)
1. **Trigger:** reserve study (Civil §5550, full visual study ≥ every 3 yrs) forecasts component repair, OR an **SB 326/721 inspection** flags urgent structural/waterproofing repair.
2. **CM/specifier** hired by board: site review → writes specification → selects bidders → oversees (multi-week bidding).
3. **RFP to a pre-qualified bid list:** CAI best practice "at least three bids"; boards not required to take lowest.
4. **Bid comparison** on price + licensure + insurance + references + scope compliance.
5. **Funding:** reserves, special assessment (member vote), and/or bank loan (banks require current reserve study, finance ~70–75%).
6. **Board approves → Notice to Proceed → CM oversees to spec.**
- **Implication:** winning spec'd CM-run work requires TWO relationships (mgmt company/board AND CM/specifier) + being on approved-vendor lists. Referrals from CAMs are decisive.

## 5. Regulatory tailwinds — SB 326 / SB 721 (VERIFIED, the demand spine)
**SB 326 — Civil Code §5551 (HOAs/condos/CIDs):** buildings 3+ attached units with wood-supported exterior elevated elements (balconies/decks/stairs/walkways) >6 ft. Inspector = licensed structural/civil engineer or architect. **"First inspection shall be completed by January 1, 2025, and then every nine years thereafter in coordination with the reserve study inspection."** NOT extended by AB 2579. Damage → report to board + incorporate into reserve study; safety threat → restrict access until repaired. **No daily fine for HOAs.**
**SB 721 — H&S §17973 (apartments/rentals):** 3+ units, wood elevated elements >6 ft (CIDs carved out → SB 326). Inspector = architect / civil-structural engineer / A,B,C-5 contractor (5+ yrs) / certified inspector. **"Inspection shall be completed by January 1, 2026, and by January 1 every six years thereafter"** (AB 2579 extended one year). Non-emergency: permit within 120 days, repair within 120 days of permit. **Penalty $100–$500/day + recordable lien — SB 721 apartments ONLY.**
**⚠️ Keep straight:** (1) daily penalty + windows = SB 721 ONLY; SB 326 HOAs have no daily fine. (2) AB 2579 extended SB 721 only (to Jan 1 2026) and excluded HOAs; SB 326 stayed Jan 1 2025. Most-mis-stated facts in the space — state exactly.
**Demand chain:** inspectors DIAGNOSE (deteriorated framing, dry rot, failed waterproofing/flashing, unsafe railings) but generally do NOT reconstruct. HOAs/owners must then hire **reconstruction/waterproofing/deck-balcony/railing contractors** under permit. Recurring (9-yr SB326 / 6-yr SB721) → demand regenerates each cycle. **Rayco's service lines map almost exactly onto the mandated repair scope, and Rayco already markets SB 326 services.** Repairs flow into the reserve study → budgeted, reserve-funded capital projects.
