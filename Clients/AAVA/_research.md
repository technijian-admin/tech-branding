## §06 — Regulatory / legal environment (the distinctive section spine; well-sourced)

The honest framing: AI is highly valuable on three SAFE axes — leasing speed, resident experience, operations —
but must be Fair-Housing-safe, privacy-safe, disclosed as a bot, and must **not** touch rent-setting.

1. **Fair Housing (federal FHA + CA FEHA + Unruh).** Two **HUD guidance documents, May 2, 2024**, apply the FHA
   to AI: (a) **tenant screening** — automated tools must comply, criteria transparent + disputable, the
   provider remains liable for outsourced/automated decisions; (b) **housing advertising** — algorithmic ad
   *audience selection* can cause digital steering / disparate impact even without intent. Three liability
   surfaces: ad targeting, screening, and **chatbots** (must give uniform answers/availability to all
   prospects; a bot that quotes different terms, ghosts some inquiries, or answers steering-type questions
   creates §3604(c) / steering exposure). CA layers FEHA + Unruh (broader protected classes) + ICRAA (limits
   on screening reports). **Design rule:** uniform scripted responses, log interactions, no demographic
   inference, AI never makes the accept/deny decision autonomously — a human owns screening on consistent criteria.

2. **Algorithmic RENT-PRICING antitrust — THE LANDMINE TO AVOID.** **United States v. RealPage** — DOJ civil
   antitrust suit **filed Aug 23, 2024** (M.D.N.C.), Sherman Act §§1–2, alleging the revenue-management software
   let landlords share nonpublic data and align rents; **California was a co-plaintiff.** Proposed settlement
   **~Nov 24, 2025** (RealPage to stop using nonpublic competitor data for price recs; no admission). **City
   bans** on rent-pricing software: San Francisco (Sept 2024, up to $1,000/violation), Philadelphia, Minneapolis
   (Mar 2025), Berkeley (Apr 2025, implementation ~Mar 2026), San Diego (May 2025, $1,000/violation), + Jersey
   City, Providence, Santa Monica, Hoboken, Portland, Seattle, Spokane, King County. **California STATEWIDE:
   AB 325** (signed Oct 6, 2025; **effective Jan 1, 2026**) amended the **Cartwright Act** — unlawful to use/
   distribute a "common pricing algorithm" as part of an agreement in restraint of trade. **Report must state
   explicitly: Technijian does NOT provide or integrate algorithmic rent-setting / revenue management; pricing
   stays with the operator.** (Flag: AB 325's reach to single-operator non-collusive use is still being
   interpreted — safe posture is to avoid competitor-data rent algorithms entirely.)

3. **Consumer privacy (CCPA/CPRA).** Governs resident/applicant PII. Applies at thresholds (~$25M+ revenue or
   high-volume processing); smaller landlords may fall below — **verify the operator's revenue (discovery).**
   If covered: privacy notice, consumer rights, "Do Not Sell/Share," sensitive-PII limits, processor DPAs. AI
   that ingests applicant data must sit behind a CCPA-compliant notice + DPA, minimize sensitive data, and not
   "sell/share" prospect data. **Selling point:** a Technijian-built solution where the operator OWNS the data.

4. **CA chatbot disclosure.** **SB 1001 (Bot Disclosure Law, operative Jul 1, 2019)** — must clearly disclose
   the user is talking to a bot when used to incentivize a transaction. (SB 243 companion-chatbot law, eff.
   Jan 1, 2026, likely outside transactional-leasing scope but reinforces disclosure; AB 2013 training-data
   transparency = developer/vendor obligation; SB 942 CA AI Transparency Act, delayed to Aug 2, 2026, large
   GenAI platforms only — awareness items, not operator obligations.) **Design rule:** the assistant opens
   with "Hi, I'm an automated assistant for The Aventine," logs the disclosure, and hands off to a human easily.

5. **CA rent regulation (awareness only — AI must not touch pricing).** **AB 1482 (Tenant Protection Act)** —
   statewide cap 5% + regional CPI (hard cap 10%/yr), just-cause after 12 months, through Jan 1, 2030; the
   Aventine (built 1997, >15 yrs old) is **covered**, not exempt. **Costa-Hawkins** preserves vacancy decontrol;
   Prop 33 (2024) repeal attempt failed. Double reason AI stays out of pricing: antitrust + rent-reg sensitivity.

## §08 — Buyer (renter) personas

Renter segments the leasing + retention engine must capture and keep. Matrix axes: X = Acquisition Volume /
Velocity, Y = Resident Lifetime Value (tenure × renewal likelihood × rent).

- **A. The Relocating Professional** — moving to the South-OC / Irvine-Spectrum job corridor; high-intent,
  compressed timeline, often out-of-area (can't walk in). Wants fast response + virtual tour + online
  application. **High volume, upper-mid LTV.** The speed-to-lead persona — where 24/7 AI wins the lease.
- **B. The Move-Up / Right-Sizing Renter** — local, upgrading into a luxury community; comparison-shops
  Apartments.com and **reads reviews heavily** (the 3.0★ hurts here). **Mid volume, mid LTV.** Reputation persona.
- **C. The Renewal-Risk Resident** — a current resident at lease-end; the cheapest "lease" to win. Sensitive to
  rent increase + service consistency (maintenance follow-through, deposit fairness — the exact pain points).
  **Low acquisition volume (retained, not acquired), HIGHEST LTV.** Retention is the quiet profit center.
- **D. The Lifestyle / Amenity Renter** — young professional or couple, drawn by resort amenities + community
  (+ pets, pending policy); active on reviews/social. **Mid-high volume, mid LTV.**
- *(Emerging) Corporate / Mid-Term Renter* — relocation/insurance/furnished housing; low volume, mid LTV.
