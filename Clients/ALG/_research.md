## §06 — Regulatory / trade environment (the distinctive section spine; well-sourced)

This is the legitimate moat. Four high-confidence regulatory + trade vectors compound at once.

1. **US import tariffs on Indian basmati — REAL VOLATILITY in 2025–2026.** Per Indian News
   Network, the Indian Rice Exporters Federation, and trade press (Open the Magazine, Rice News
   Today, StratNews Global): **Indian rice imports faced a base 10% tariff in early 2025; it
   escalated to 50%; settled at 18% after the India–US trade deal in early 2026.** Thai jasmine
   shipments sit at ~19%, Pakistani basmati ~19%. From April–November 2025, Indian basmati to the
   US ran 199,558 tonnes worth ~Rs 1,749 crore (~$210M USD-equivalent). **Translation:** the
   margin / cost-of-goods on ALG's largest product category just moved a few hundred basis points
   inside one year, with more political risk on the calendar. AI that monitors tariff signals,
   reformulates buyer cost models, and proactively proposes origin-mix shifts (India → Thailand
   → Pakistan → US-domestic blend) is a defensible capability AND a reason to call procurement
   directors right now. **Source as discovery items, do not over-claim specific basis-point
   savings without ALG's actual book.**
2. **FDA FSMA 204 Food Traceability Rule (Section 204).** The original compliance date was
   **2026-01-20**. In August 2025 the FDA proposed a 30-month extension, and the November 2025
   Continuing Appropriations Act directed the FDA not to enforce before **2028-07-20**. The
   rule's substance is unchanged — record-keeping for foods on the FDA's Food Traceability List
   (cheeses, shell eggs, fruits & vegetables, seafood, ready-to-eat deli salads — note: rice
   itself is NOT currently on the FTL; **IQF vegetables ARE in the produce categories likely to
   be covered depending on type**, confirm by SKU). **Even though deadline is now 2028, top
   retailers are pre-flighting traceability requirements in supplier agreements NOW.** Buying
   into FSMA-204-ready supplier capability *before* it's mandatory is a retailer competitive
   move. Translation: ALG should be FSMA-204-credentialed in conversation **today**, regardless
   of the new enforcement date.
3. **Foreign Supplier Verification Program (FSVP) and HARPC — already mandatory.** Because ALG
   imports from India and Thailand, FSVP applies to the US importer of record; HARPC/preventive
   controls apply at the Irvine/NJ handling facilities. Every retail / industrial buyer audits
   these documents. AI Document Intelligence (Technijian's proven FINRA build) is purpose-built
   for it.
4. **USDA NOP organic chain of custody.** ALG holds the CCOF NOP processor-handler certificate
   (PR2333, 2020-04-29; US/Canada Equivalence 2023-07-28). The NOP record-keeping and
   segregation rules are non-trivial for a multi-origin operator. AI that maintains the digital
   chain of custody (input certificates → lot → output product → buyer COA) reduces the audit
   surface.
5. **Buyer-mandated certifications (awareness — confirm what ALG already holds).** Most national
   retail / foodservice / industrial buyers require one of GFSI's recognized standards (BRC /
   SQF / FSSC 22000) plus, for relevant SKUs, kosher (OU / OK / Star-K) and halal (IFANCA /
   ISA / HMA) certificates. Parent BIEL holds BRC at the mill; verify Algro's US-facility holds.

The honest framing for the report's §03 spine: **AI is highly valuable on three SAFE axes — buyer
documentation velocity, multi-origin compliance / traceability, and trade/tariff signal
monitoring — and is explicitly not used to (a) override a human food-safety determination,
(b) substitute for a GFSI audit, or (c) make pricing decisions in any antitrust-sensitive way
(no shared-data rent-pricing analog).**

ABM personas — distinct buyer types in the *named-account* universe. Matrix axes: X = ACCOUNT
VOLUME / VELOCITY of opportunities at that buyer type, Y = ACCOUNT LIFETIME VALUE (revenue per
won account × tenure × renewal likelihood).

- **A. The Retail Private-Label Brand Manager** — at Costco (Kirkland), Walmart (Great Value),
  Kroger (Private Selection / Simple Truth), Target (Good & Gather), Whole Foods (365), Sprouts,
  Aldi, Wegmans. Owns one or more rice/grain SKUs on a 12–24-month award cycle. Pain points:
  RFP-cycle speed, FSMA-readiness, kosher/halal/organic chain of custody, packaging-design
  partnership, willingness to take on a sustainability scorecard. **Mid volume, HIGH LTV** —
  single-PL award can be a multi-year, multi-million-dollar relationship.
- **B. The Industrial / CPG Procurement Buyer** — at Conagra, Nestle, Kraft Heinz, General Mills,
  Amy's Kitchen, Saffron Road, Tattooed Chef, Maya Kaimal, regional frozen-meal co-packers.
  Buys rice as an *ingredient* into a downstream branded SKU. Pain points: spec consistency by
  lot, on-time delivery to manufacturing schedule, capacity flex on seasonal SKU launches,
  willingness to participate in recipe development. **High volume of opportunities, mid-HIGH
  LTV** per won account. Highest-velocity persona.
- **C. The Foodservice Operator / Distributor Category Buyer** — at Sysco / US Foods / PFG /
  UNFI / KeHE on the distribution side; at restaurant chains (Chipotle / Cava / Sweetgreen /
  Halal Guys / Cheesecake Factory / Texas Roadhouse / Olive Garden / Yum / Panera / P.F. Chang's
  / Saffron Road's foodservice arm) on the operator side. Pain points: per-SKU spec sheets in
  GS1/GDSN format, price-hold reliability through tariff swings, fast hold/recall coordination,
  same-day SPOC. **High volume, mid LTV** per account (operators reorder constantly but switch
  on price).
- **D. The Specialty / Ethnic / Mission-Driven Brand Owner** — at growing CPG brands (e.g.
  Saffron Road, Maya Kaimal, Tasty Bite, Stonefire, Kitchens of India), restaurant groups, or
  retailer specialty arms. Wants a co-pack partner who can do organic + halal + kosher + Indian
  + Thai + custom recipe iteration. Pain points: minimum-order flexibility, recipe-development
  collaboration, brand-storytelling partnership. **Mid volume, mid LTV** — but the highest fit
  for ALG's specialty range (the "right" partner for them is rare).
- **E. The Institutional / Channel Buyer** *(emerging, confirm in discovery)* — K-12 nutrition,
  federal commodities, military commissaries, healthcare contract foodservice (Sodexo / Aramark
  / Compass). Pain points: USDA / Buy-American compliance, paperwork volume, audit posture.
  **Low volume, high LTV** (multi-year contracts).

Personas A and B are the highest-value targets — and exactly where AI Document Intelligence +
ABM intelligence + AEO-authority compound.
