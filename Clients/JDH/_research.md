## §02 — How a contract manufacturer / supply-chain partner wins

Revenue is not built on consumer marketing; it is built on a finite, named universe of industrial
OEM accounts, won and kept through a multi-stage account motion:

1. **GET SPECIFIED / DISCOVERED** — A sourcing manager or NPI engineer at an OEM needs a casting,
   forging, or machined part — for a new product, a cost-down, a re-source off a failing supplier,
   or a tariff-driven supply-chain rebalance. They find candidate suppliers via referral, trade
   relationships, a supplier-diversity program, or — increasingly — an AI/search query.
2. **RFQ / QUOTE** — The OEM sends a drawing + spec + volume; the supplier returns a quote, lead
   time, and capability evidence. **Speed and completeness here decide the award.**
3. **QUALIFY** — Supplier qualification: PPAP / FAI / AS9102, ISO / IATF audit, material certs,
   capacity review, sometimes a site audit. Document-heavy and slow.
4. **AWARD / PO** — First PO, often a pilot lot, then a blanket / Kanban supply agreement.
5. **DELIVER & SCORECARD** — On-time delivery, PPM defect rate, COA on every lot; the OEM keeps a
   supplier scorecard that governs whether the next program comes your way.
6. **HOLD / GROW** — Re-orders, the next part on the same platform, the next platform, and the
   next plant. The cheapest revenue in the business is the re-quote and the next-part-on-the-
   same-account — and it is the easiest to lose to thin memory or a slow response.

**Where the cheapest wins live:** (a) the re-quote / next-part on an account already won — near-
zero acquisition cost; (b) the supplier-diversity slot JDH qualifies for as an MBE but isn't
surfaced into; (c) the program lost today purely on quote speed, not capability or price.

## §03 — The trade-compliance & quality-integrity boundary (the JDH analog of VAF/AAVA/ALG)

This is the section that earns credibility. A manufacturer-importer that deploys AI across
quoting, classification, and quality docs without naming the boundary loses any sophisticated
OEM's supplier-quality or trade-compliance reviewer the first time they ask. The boundary is
simple to state: **AI drafts, classifies, assembles, and monitors — a qualified human signs.**

### Trade compliance (the import side)

- **Section 301 (China)** tariffs remain in force and escalated through 2025; many castings/
  forgings sit on the lists. **Section 232** (steel/aluminum) and **AD/CVD** orders (antidumping /
  countervailing duties — iron castings and certain forged components from China have a real
  AD/CVD history) add stacking duty exposure.
- **CBP substantial-transformation doctrine** governs country of origin: simply trans-shipping
  Chinese components through Mexico for minor finishing does NOT change origin or escape Section
  301. Real manufacturing transformation does. JDH's Mexico and India operations have to add
  genuine value to claim origin — and the honest sales story respects that.
- **Mexico is not a guaranteed safe harbor:** USTR opened a NEW Section 301 investigation into
  Mexico on 2026-03-11 (determinations expected ~2026-07-24), and Mexico's Dec 2025 decree
  expanded import duties on 1,463 tariff lines. The 2026 sourcing answer is data-driven
  total-landed-cost + origin flexibility, not a single-country bet.
- **The boundary:** AI classifies parts (HTS), models landed cost by origin, and monitors policy
  changes — but a **licensed customs broker / JDH's trade-compliance owner signs the entry and the
  country-of-origin declaration.** AI never fabricates an origin claim or an HTS classification it
  can't source.

### Quality-system integrity (the delivery side)

- AI assembles PPAP / FAI / cert packages and drafts COAs **from real inspection data** (the
  PC-DMIS / CMM dimensional results, material certs, test reports). It **never fabricates an
  inspection result, a material certification, or a dimensional report.** A **quality engineer
  signs.** The CMM data is the source of truth; the AI is the assembler and the memory, not the
  inspector.

### No price-fixing / no collusion (the RealPage doctrine by analogy)

- AI informs JDH's OWN should-cost and quote from public cost data and JDH's own history. It does
  NOT share pricing across competitors or feed any multi-firm rate-setting engine. (Same line we
  draw in AAVA and DTS — the US DOJ v. RealPage doctrine imported by analogy.)

**Stated plainly:** *AI drafts the quote, classifies the part, assembles the cert package, and
watches the tariff — a JDH engineer signs the material cert and a licensed broker signs the
customs entry. AI never fabricates an inspection result or a country-of-origin claim.* That
boundary is the point: JDH gets institutional-grade quoting and compliance velocity without
putting its name on a number an algorithm invented or an origin a model guessed.

## §08 — The silent margin leak (where revenue walks out of a contract manufacturer)

1. **The quotes that went out slow — or never went out.** Engineering bandwidth caps how many
   RFQs get a full, fast, complete quote. The hard/complex ones get deferred; the OEM awards to
   whoever quoted first. Every slow or skipped quote is a parts program lost to a faster
   competitor, not a better one. AI RFQ intelligence + quote-history recall removes the ceiling
   without adding headcount.
2. **The supplier-diversity slots JDH qualifies for but never gets surfaced into.** As an NMSDC
   MBE, JDH is exactly what Fortune-500 Tier-1/Tier-2 diverse-spend programs are looking for — but
   only if it's in the channel with audit-ready docs. Diversity spend that JDH leaves on the table
   is the highest-margin revenue in the business, lost silently because no one works the channel.
3. **The re-order / re-quote lost to thin memory — and the margin lost to tariff surprises.** A
   repeat part gets re-quoted from scratch (or lost) because the prior quote, tooling, and cost
   history live in someone's spreadsheet or head. And when a tariff or duty shifts and nobody
   catches it, a quoted job ships at a margin that evaporated between quote and PO. AI quote-
   history memory + a tariff/landed-cost monitor close both leaks.
