## 4. The strategic spine — WHY THIS, WHY NOW (the thesis)
**CAG's product is de-risking and de-frictioning China sourcing for automotive buyers. In 2026 that job is harder, costlier, and more valuable than at any point in their history — and AI lets them do it dramatically faster and more precisely.** The trade environment is the dominant force in their world, and it cuts both ways:
- **Threat:** tariffs/volatility push some buyers to leave China or self-source; margin pressure everywhere.
- **Opportunity (bigger):** the harder sourcing gets, the more buyers need an expert, US-accountable partner who can classify parts, model landed cost across countries, vet/diversify suppliers, and own the customs/duty risk. CAG's "Landed Your Warehouse" + importer-of-record + "no contingent duty liability" model is *exactly* what a tariff-era buyer wants. AI is the force-multiplier on every step.

## 5. Industry & trade landscape (SOURCED — Section 03 of the report)
- **Section 301, List 3** covers auto parts at **25%** (imposed Sept 2018 at 10%, raised to **25%** in May/June 2019). Source: USTR; **Auto Care Association** (autocare.org Section 301 page).
- Section 301 duties **stack on top of the MFN/base rate** (e.g., a 2.5% MFN part on List 3 → ~27.5% total). **EVs raised to 100% (2024); EV lithium-ion batteries to 25%.** **178 product exclusions valid until Nov 10, 2026.**
- Broader **2025–26 US-China tariff environment is elevated and volatile** (additional IEEPA/"reciprocal" actions on top of 301). **Frame as "elevated and volatile," do NOT cite a single precise total stack** — it has shifted repeatedly and would be a truthfulness risk. Anchor on the durable, well-documented **25% Section 301 List 3** figure + the stacking principle.
- **China+1 diversification** (Vietnam, India, Thailand, Mexico) is now the dominant procurement-risk strategy — directly relevant to CAG's multi-country sourcing value.
- **Market context (label as industry, not CAG's number):** the U.S. auto care industry is large — the **Auto Care Association** sizes the total industry in the hundreds of billions of dollars — and a substantial share of replacement parts/components is imported, much of it from China. Do NOT fabricate a precise market-size figure.
- **Compliance/QC frame:** PPAP / APQP / IATF 16949 (TS16949 successor) / AD-CVD (anti-dumping / countervailing duty) / HTS classification = the documentation burden AI can automate into audit-ready records.
- **Guardrail (sourced):** customs entry / HTS classification is legally the **importer's responsibility** (CBP reasonable care). A wrong classification = duty underpayment, penalties, and liability → **AI drafts/screens the classification; a qualified human confirms every entry.** (Mirrors the human-in-the-loop principle.)

## 7. AI opportunity inventory (two fronts; integration-rich)
**Growth (ABM):** authority/AEO content for buyer questions; account & trigger intelligence on target parts brands; RFQ/proposal auto-drafting; reputation/case-study capture.
**Integration / efficiency (the easier yes — saves hours on work they do daily):**
- **Supplier discovery & risk-scoring** — AI mines/normalizes their factory database + open sources, filters and risk-scores candidates (extends "database of hundreds of factories").
- **HTS classification + multi-country landed-cost modeling** — classify a part to its HTS code, model duty + freight + landed cost across China / Vietnam / Mexico / India → the engine behind "Landed Your Warehouse," China+1 advisory, and "no contingent duty liability." **Highest-conviction, most CAG-specific play.**
- **RFQ / quote / spec drafting** — turn a buyer's loose requirement into a structured RFQ, spec sheet, and quote in minutes.
- **Quality-doc automation (PPAP / IATF 16949 / inspection reports)** — assemble audit-ready quality + customs paperwork (Jacky's QC + import admin).
- **Production / order tracking** — AI layer over Acctivate: flag at-risk POs, schedule slips, container status.
- **East-West customer comms + translation** — draft status updates, translate China↔US factory/customer threads, 24/7 coverage.
- **Institutional-knowledge graph** — capture 70 yrs of supplier/part/program know-how before key people retire (succession/onboarding).
- **Guardrail throughout:** human signs off on any classification, quote, or commitment that moves money or carries customs/compliance liability; proprietary buyer specs + supplier pricing stay in private, governed deployments (never public AI tools).

## 9. Competitive landscape (REAL names, by type — Section 06)
1. **Generalist China sourcing agencies** — **Dragon Sourcing** (Shanghai; 12 countries; OEM/ODM incl. automotive brake-pad category; factory-risk tools), **Leeline Sourcing** (Shenzhen; 6,000+ clients; ~5–10% commission), Supplyia, Jingsourcing. China-domiciled, category-agnostic, generally **not** the US importer of record.
2. **B2B marketplaces / DIY-direct** — **Alibaba, Made-in-China.com, Global Sources.** Cheapest, broadest — but the buyer owns **all** the risk (trading-company-not-factory, QC, language, customs, HTS classification, tariff/AD-CVD exposure). The real "do-nothing/DIY" alternative CAG's value defends against.
3. **Quality-inspection firms** — **QIMA (Asia Quality Focus), SGS, Bureau Veritas, TÜV.** One slice (inspection) — not turnkey sourcing + engineering + import + finance.
4. **Freight forwarders / customs brokers w/ sourcing add-ons** — logistics + customs, not factory sourcing/engineering.
5. **In-house China buying office** — large buyers build their own; high control but high fixed cost + they own the QC/tariff risk.
**White space CAG owns:** US-domiciled **importer of record** + **automotive specialization** + **engineering** + **in-China QC** + **landed-cost pricing** + **financial de-risking** (USD/open-account/no-L/C/no-contingent-duty). Few combine US-entity accountability + auto depth + full turnkey. Competitive matrix axes: **Turnkey accountability / risk transferred to partner × Automotive specialization & engineering depth** — CAG in the open top-right corner.
