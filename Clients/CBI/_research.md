## 02 — The Two-Channel Model (non-obvious — worth the model diagram)
- **Homebuilder channel (B2B):** Builder outsources the **design center** → CBI runs buyer personalization (online via **Studio Chateau** wish-lists + **≥4 designer meetings** + a post-move-in walkthrough) → installs flooring/finishes. Pitch: **"turn builders into New Home Retailers"** / "help builders sell more homes + lift option margin" (built-to-order options run ~3-5 margin pts above inventory for builders). Win a builder = recurring buyer flow across a community. **Account-based.**
- **Homeowner channel (B2C):** Homeowner finds CBI via **local search / reviews / showroom / shop-at-home** → design consult + flooring/finishes selection → install. Remodel-heavy, LVP-dominant. **Demand-gen.**
- Revenue center of gravity: **B2C flooring/remodel = the base; builder design-center = the higher-margin growth bet.**

## 03 — The Market & Where It's Going (2024-2026)
- **SD new-home construction:** City of San Diego permitted **~8,782 homes in 2024** (2nd-best of the decade; ~9,200/yr recent, +40% over the cycle); 2024 SFR starts +46%, **2025 ~flat** (plateau). BUT new construction is only **~2-3% of SD home sales** → builder channel is high-value-per-deal but lumpy; **remodel/B2C is the larger, steadier base.**
- **Flooring market:** vinyl floor-covering **$43.6B (2025) → $60.4B (2030), 6.7% CAGR**; **LVP/LVT dominate**; **R&R = 62% of spend** vs new-construction. Renovation spending growth **slow but positive** (LIRA +2.9%→+1.6%, ~$522B by end-2026); rate cut = upside.
- **Homebuilder design-center / personalization trend:** options are a builder **profit center**; **online option-selection software is table stakes** (Studio Chateau, builder studios) with virtual room designers + virtual appointments. The narrative builders buy: personalization sells more homes + lifts margin.
- **How homeowners choose (local home-services reality):** **91% rely on online reviews**; discovery order = **Google Search/Maps + reviews lead** (56% Google reviews, 51% the site, 43% Yelp, 36% FB, 9% Houzz); **visual platforms (IG/Pinterest/Houzz) shape the shortlist** for visual categories like flooring/design; **referrals close 50-70% but referred buyers still Google + review-scan + visit the site.** Winners own: GBP + review volume/velocity, a deep visual portfolio across web/Houzz/IG/Pinterest, city×product local pages, and **instant visualization.**
