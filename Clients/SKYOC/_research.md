## §06 — Industry & market dynamics (the "why now")

- **The market is big and growing.** US B2B trade shows: **$15.8B (2024) → $17.3B (2028)**; global
  market pushing toward ~$50B. **72%** of B2B buyers still prefer meeting vendors in person — the
  show floor isn't going away; it's getting more competitive for attention.
- **AI is the dominant 2026 trend.** ~**50%** of event professionals now use AI tools in some part
  of their trade-show operations (up from ~25% in 2024); AI booth activations report up to **+45%**
  engagement; AI badge/business-card scanning exceeds **95%** accuracy; interactive elements keep
  attendees **2–3×** longer; digital lead capture converts **3×** better than paper.
- **Spend is shifting** to the three areas AI most affects: **booth design, pre-show marketing, and
  post-show follow-up.** Per-attendee cost is rising ~2.4%, so exhibitors demand measurable ROI —
  exactly what an AI engagement + measurement layer provides.
- **The parent is moving** (CMX / 2D2A / 10+ digital activations under PE ownership). The dealers
  who add the local managed-AI layer ride that wave; the ones who stay "a booth vendor" get
  commoditized.
- **Light compliance boundary (note, don't lead).** Trade-show lead capture collects attendee PII
  (badge scans, business cards) → **CCPA/CPRA** (consumer data), **CAN-SPAM** (post-show email),
  **TCPA** (if texting), and **CA SB 1001** bot-disclosure (a booth chatbot must disclose it's a
  bot). The honest framing: **AI assists capture, enrichment, and follow-up; it does not make
  consent or legal determinations, and it never replaces the designer's creative judgment or the
  human relationship.** Stating the boundary builds credibility — it is light here vs. lending/
  housing clients, but real.
