## 3. Customer voice / buyer reality

A design engineer with a problem (integrate a discrete analog circuit into one chip; shrink BOM/size/power; protect
IP; secure long-term supply; **replace a dead/obsolete part**) is a **high-intent researcher**. They search very
specific technical terms and increasingly **ask an AI assistant first** ("who designs custom mixed-signal ASICs for
medical devices in the US?", "drop-in replacement for an obsolete high-voltage gate-driver IC"). Whether CSS is the
cited answer is the whole game. CSS's credibility (100% tape-out success, 25+ yr engineers, own test floor, US-based,
fab-agnostic, obsolescence niche) is **strong but nearly invisible online**.

  Silicon Systems (UK), AnSem (Belgium), ICsense (Belgium, TI-owned, 170+ tape-outs), SystematIC (NL), EnSilica (UK),
  Cyient Semiconductors.
- **Larger / structured-ASIC:** Renesas (custom/structured ASIC). **Context-only giants:** Broadcom, Marvell — custom
  ASIC at **AI-accelerator scale** (Google TPU, Meta MTIA), a *different niche* from CSS's analog/mixed-signal/sensor,
  low-to-mid-volume, long-lifecycle, obsolescence work. Frame honestly — CSS is NOT chasing AI-accelerator silicon.
- **The real DIY alternative:** the buyer builds it from discrete components or settles for an off-the-shelf standard
  IC. CSS's counter = integration, IP protection, supply security, obsolescence rescue.

**Competitive axes (2×2):** *US turnkey accountability (design → in-house test → production)* × *analog/mixed-signal &
high-voltage specialization.* CSS sits **top-right** (US-domiciled, fab-agnostic, own test floor, 100% tape-out
success, obsolescence niche) — but with **near-zero digital/AI-search visibility**. White space = that corner is
hard to copy; AI widens the moat. Offshore boutiques are deep but not US-accountable; big-ASIC houses won't take
low/mid-volume analog work; marketplaces/standard-IC transfer no design help.
