## 04 — Industry Context (2025–2026, sourced)

- **Market:** US Pet Grooming & Boarding **$15.4B (2026)**; US Pet Care & Services **$66.3B (2026) → $91.7B by 2031** (6.7% CAGR); US pet **daycare $1.4B → $2.94B by 2035** (8.6% CAGR); pet-tech **$19B → $53B** by 2035 (~12% CAGR). Services are the fastest-growing major pet segment. (IBISWorld; Mordor; MarkWide)
- **Humanization/premiumization:** ~90% of owners view pets as family; "resort" positioning, live-stream cams, spa services are now premium table-stakes. (Clarkston; IBPSA)
- **Digital expectations:** **84%** of pet owners say online booking is important; **89%** want digital reminders; **85%** want post-stay summaries; **88% of under-35 owners** check reviews; **48%** are likely to switch providers within a year — **slow communication outweighs clinical outcomes** as a switch driver. (Vetstoria; PetDesk)
- **Operating economics:** labor ≈ **45% of gross revenue** and is the #1 risk (turnover kills consistency); facilities target **60–70% weekday occupancy** to break even at **$25–$40/dog-day**. Membership/recurring revenue and automation are the margin levers. (Gingr; MoeGo; Financial Models Lab)
- **AI adoption:** booking/ops software cuts admin **~40%** and lifts satisfaction **~25%**; AI can handle **~79%** of routine customer comms; **dynamic pricing** is spreading; **AEO** — Gartner projects **25% of organic search shifts to AI chatbots by 2026**, and Google AI Overviews now name specific local businesses. (DaySmart; Anolla; Mordor; HubSpot)
