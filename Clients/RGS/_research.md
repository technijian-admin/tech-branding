## 2. Regulatory Obligations (FACTS about the frameworks — sourced; NOT claims about ROG's compliance state)

### SSA Representative Payee Program
- **Annual accounting:** payees file a **Representative Payee Report** at least yearly — **SSA-623** (organizational payees). Legal basis **20 CFR 404.2065**. Org payees are NOT exempt.
- **Recordkeeping:** keep bank statements, canceled checks, receipts; **2-year retention**; produce on demand. **20 CFR 404.2035.**
- **Separate accounting / no commingling:** conserved funds titled in a **fiduciary** capacity; organizational payees MAY use a **collective account** but must NOT commingle with org funds and must keep a **per-beneficiary ledger** of deposits/expenditures/transfers. **20 CFR 404.2045** + POMS GN 00603.020. *(Implies a per-beneficiary system of record.)*
- **Conserved funds:** beneficiary's property; SSI resource limits **$2,000 individual / $3,000 couple** (eligibility risk), SSI back-pay generally spent down in 9 months — balances must be monitored. *(SSI figures adjust — verify live.)*
- **SSA monitoring:** **periodic onsite reviews** of organizational payees (largely performed by state **Protection & Advocacy** systems) + triggered reviews.
- **Organizational-payee fee:** lesser of **10% of monthly benefit or $57** (general) / **$106** (DAA cases), as of Jan 2026, COLA-adjusted. *(Verify on live SSA page each January.)*

### VA Fiduciary Program
- **Annual accounting:** **VA Form 21P-4706b** (21P-4706c court-appointed). Required when funds under management **> $10,000**, fiduciary takes a fee, beneficiary rated 100% disabled, or Hub Manager requires — due within 30 days of period end. **38 CFR 13.280.**
- **Separate account per beneficiary:** must establish a **separate federally-insured account for EACH VA beneficiary**, titled in both names, **no commingling**. **38 CFR 13.200.**
- **Oversight:** VA **field examinations (38 CFR 13.120)** and **onsite reviews (38 CFR 13.300)**. Recordkeeping 13.140; bonding 13.230; misuse 13.400. Governing: **38 CFR Part 13.**

### Data protection (this is the honest, differentiated part)
- **SSA PII-safeguarding requirement — FACT, applies to payees:** SSA expressly directs payees to safeguard beneficiary PII (name, DOB, **SSN**, Medicare number, **bank account info**, address, **health records**, payment data), to have **"documented procedures … that protect personal information,"** extend them to volunteers/contractors, and apply **role-based access**. (ssa.gov/kc/rp_pii.htm; ssa.gov/payee/protecting_personal_info.htm)
- **OPEN QUESTIONS — flag, do NOT assert (require legal review):**
  - **GLBA / FTC Safeguards Rule (16 CFR 314):** whether a rep-payee nonprofit is a "financial institution" is unsettled — do NOT assert it applies.
  - **CCPA/CPRA:** applies to a for-profit "business"; CA AG says it does NOT include nonprofits — likely exempt, but confirm (affiliate/branding edge cases).
  - **HIPAA:** a payee/fiduciary is generally NOT a covered entity, but ROG clearly receives health info (clients' mental illness/disability; "helps obtain medical treatment"). Triggers to test: does ROG bill Medi-Cal/health claims electronically, or act as a business associate? **California CMIA** (Civ. Code §56) may reach medical info regardless.
- **CA Professional Fiduciaries Bureau licensing — FACT:** rep payees fall OUTSIDE the "professional fiduciary" definition (BPC §6501); a 501(c)(3) is also excluded (§6501(f)(4)(D)). Only triggered if ROG/staff also serve as court-appointed conservator/guardian/trustee/POA-agent. Role-specific — discovery question if they do anything beyond payee/VA-fiduciary work.

## 3. Threat Landscape (sourced MARKET facts — NOT claims about ROG)

**BEC / payment diversion (most relevant — ROG disburses funds + loads prepaid cards):**
- **$2.77B** BEC losses to FBI IC3 in 2024 (21,442 complaints). IC3 specifically flags **payroll/payment-diversion to attacker-controlled prepaid cards** as a BEC subtype. (IC3 2024; PSA I-091124-PSA)
- **$16.6B** total IC3 cybercrime losses 2024 (+33% YoY).

**Ransomware (small orgs / nonprofits):**
- **59%** of orgs hit in the prior year; **$2M** avg ransom; **$2.73M** avg recovery cost ex-ransom. (Sophos State of Ransomware 2024)
- **88%** of SMB breaches involved ransomware/extortion vs 39% at large orgs. (Verizon 2025 DBIR)
- ~21–24 days typical downtime. (Coveware/Statista — directional)

**Elder / vulnerable-adult & benefit fraud (ROG's population):**
- **$4.885B** losses by victims 60+ to IC3 in 2024 (+43% YoY; 147,127 complaints). (IC3 Elder Fraud 2024)
- **$2.4B** older-adult fraud losses to FTC 2024 (~4× 2020). (FTC, Dec 2025)
- **SSA OIG:** $322M recovered + 833 convictions FY24–H1 FY25; an Oct 2023 audit found 144 payee-misuse cases, no corrective action on 135. (SSA OIG)
- **GAO-19-688:** organizational-payee beneficiaries (~1M people) "among the most vulnerable" to exploitation; weak SSA oversight; **no background checks for organizational-payee employees**.

**Cost of a breach:** **$4.44M** global / **$10.22M** US avg (IBM 2025). **$3.31M** for orgs <500 employees *(secondary source — verify)*. AI/automation in security saved **$1.9M + 80 days**.

**Human element:** **60%** of breaches involved the human element; **22%** began with stolen credentials (#1 vector); phishing #1 by frequency; median <60s to fall for a phish. (Verizon 2025 DBIR; IBM 2025)

**Nonprofit-specific:** **59.2%** of nonprofits give no regular cyber training; **68.2%** have no documented incident-response plan. (NTEN 2018 — label the year.)
