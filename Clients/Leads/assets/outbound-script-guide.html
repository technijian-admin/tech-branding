1. Pre-Call Prep — 5 minutes per prospect

Before you dial, do these five things. They take 5 minutes total and double your connect rate.

  1. Open the company website. Note the header tagline, what they sell, who they sell to. Skim the Careers page for tech-stack hints (Azure, AWS, EHR, QuickBooks).
  2. LinkedIn company page. Employee count, HQ, recent posts. If <25, angle for owner/operator. If 25–200, angle for Director of IT / Ops / CFO.
  3. Google "[Company] technology" and "[Company] breach". Any news? A breach is your opener. A new CTO hire is your opener. An award is your opener.
  4. Check the industry tag in leads-classified.csv. That gives you the primary pitch, core service bundle, and MSP-interim wedges for their vertical (§4).
  5. Write down one specific observation to drop in the opener. This is what separates you from every other cold caller.

Target ratio: 8–12 dials per live conversation. Target per day: 20 meaningful conversations, 3 booked discovery meetings.

2. The Master Script — ~3 min conversation

2.1 Opener (first 15 seconds)

"Hi, this is [Your Name] with Technijian — we're a managed IT and AI services firm out of Irvine. I know I'm calling out of the blue. Do you have 60 seconds, or did I catch you at a bad time?"

Why this works: Names yourself, names the company, names where you are (local), asks permission. California SMBs hate being surprised — they respect it when you ask.

If "bad time": book a specific day + rough time. CRM it.
If "what's this about?" (most common): move to 2.2.
If "sure, what's up?": move to 2.2.

2.2 The One-Line Why

"I'm reaching out because we work with [industry] firms here in Orange County and LA — we run their IT, their cybersecurity, and increasingly their AI strategy. We keep seeing three things come up this year — [one pain relevant to their industry; see §4]. Is that something on your plate, or is IT already sorted out for you?"

Why this works: Industry-specific ("we work with construction firms") signals you're not spraying. The pain you mention is a tell — if they engage, you've qualified. If they say "IT is handled," you pivot to §3 (MSP-interim wedge).

2.3 Discovery — 2 to 4 questions max

Pick from this list. Do not ask all of them. Listen for where they engage.

  • "How are you currently handling IT today — internal team, outsourced, or a mix?"
  • "When's the last time someone did a security or compliance assessment for you?"
  • "Where does AI fit in your roadmap — or hasn't it come up yet?"
  • "Are you on Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace? Any pain there?"
  • "What's the one IT or tech thing that's nagging at you right now — the thing you'd pay to make go away?"

Listen. You're not selling yet. You're qualifying whether there's a real problem and whether they have authority/budget.

2.4 The Offer — pick ONE, don't stack

If they said...Offer this
"We handle it ourselves / one internal guy"30-min IT assessment + written report. No-cost. "We'll tell you the three things we'd fix first."
"We have an MSP"Interim wedge — see §3. Do not try to replace the MSP on call one.
"We just had a breach / audit finding / compliance issue"Nexus Assess Pulse — vulnerability + pen test. $3–8K. 2 weeks. Written remediation plan.
"We're looking at AI but don't know where to start"Chat.AI demo + AI strategy session. 60 min. No cost.

2.5 The Close — book the meeting

"Here's what I'd suggest: 30 minutes on Zoom — you bring whatever's on your mind IT-wise, I'll bring one of our senior folks who knows your industry. Worst case, you get a free hour of advice. Best case, we save you a ton of money or a ton of risk. What does next Tuesday or Thursday afternoon look like?"

Get two options on the calendar. Send the invite before you hang up. Follow-up email within 10 minutes (§7).

3. "We Already Have an MSP" — Interim Wedge Playbook

This is the most important section. 60–70% of SMBs above $5M revenue already have an MSP. Do NOT try to displace the incumbent on the first call. You'll lose the deal and waste the lead.

Instead, offer parallel-running services — services the incumbent MSP almost certainly isn't delivering. These are your wedge. Once you've delivered one of these well, displacement conversations happen naturally 12–24 months later when the MSP contract comes up for renewal.

3.1 Universal MSP-interim wedges

These apply across every industry:

Chat.AI — Private-Tenant Enterprise AI

"We're launching a multi-tenant AI platform that runs on our own infrastructure — never trains on your data, supports 8 LLM providers, with HIPAA audit + GDPR data-subject rights built in. Your MSP isn't offering this. Most clients start with a 30-day pilot."

My AI — AI Strategy Consulting

"Your MSP keeps your email running. We help you figure out where AI actually moves the needle in your business. 60-minute assessment, written roadmap, no pressure."

Nexus Assess Pulse — Vulnerability Intel + Pen Test

"This isn't what your MSP does. It's an independent, external vuln scan + pen test. You hand the report to your cyber insurance carrier at renewal. $3–8K, delivered in 2 weeks."

My Compliance — Modular Compliance Services

"HIPAA, SOC 2, PCI, GDPR, CMMC — your MSP may be 'aware' of these but doesn't run a compliance program for you. We do. We pair with your existing IT to produce audit-ready evidence."

My AI Lead Gen — AI-Driven Outbound Lead Gen

"Totally outside the IT conversation. We use public-data scraping + AI enrichment to deliver scored, tiered leads your sales team can call Monday. Ask me about the luxury-home-builder case — 24 tier-1 leads in a 75-minute run."

My Dev — AI-Assisted Software Development

"We build custom applications 3–5× faster using our SDLC v7.0 methodology (AI-native). If your business depends on a piece of software that's getting stale or needs integration, this is for you."

My SEO — Search, Content, Video, AI Search

"Your MSP doesn't touch your website. We do SEO, content, video, and AI search optimization — unlimited monthly hours, one fee. If your phone isn't ringing from Google, this is where to start."

3.2 How to introduce the wedge

After they say "we already have an MSP":

"Totally expected — and honestly, replacing your MSP isn't what I'd suggest anyway. What I'd ask is: is your MSP doing [AI / compliance / cybersecurity posture / SEO / custom dev]? Because those are the three things we hear SMBs wishing their MSP would do but it's not in scope. We run those as independent services alongside an existing IT partner all the time."

Wait for them to tell you which gap they feel most. That's your wedge.

3.3 Why this works long-term

  • Service revenue from the wedge engagement (often $15–60K/yr)
  • Relationship capital — you're the person who delivered value without stepping on their IT partner
  • Future displacement — when their MSP drops the ball (and eventually they all do), you're the first call

5. Objection Handlers

"We're not interested."

"Totally fair. Can I ask — is that because IT is handled and you're happy, or because now isn't the time? Just so I know whether to come back in a quarter or close out the file."

Listen for "handled" → §3 (MSP interim wedge). Listen for "not now" → calendar a check-in.

"Send me some information."

This is almost always a brush-off. Counter with:

"Happy to — what specifically? If you tell me what area you'd actually want to understand better, I can send something targeted. Otherwise I'm just adding to your inbox, and neither of us wants that."

Specific topic → send matching service one-pager (§8). "Just whatever" → brush-off; 90-day re-touch.

"We already have an MSP and we're happy."

"Great — we work alongside MSPs all the time. Two questions: does your MSP handle AI strategy, and do they deliver compliance evidence to your auditors or cyber-insurance carrier? Because those are the two areas where we pick up the slack for about half our clients."

Yes to both → nurture. No to either → §3.

"We're too small."

"Every client we have was too small at some point. Our smallest clients are 5 people, our biggest are 500. If IT or AI takes up more than 2 hours a week of your time personally, we can probably help. Sound familiar?"

"Budget is tight."

"Understood. Here's the deal — we do a no-cost 30-minute assessment that just tells you the three things we'd fix first. You do nothing with it, you keep the report. If there's budget later, you know where to start. If there isn't, no pressure. Worth 30 minutes?"

"I don't make the decisions on IT."

"That's actually helpful. Who would be the right person — the owner, or someone in Finance or Ops? I'll reach out to them directly so I'm not taking up your time on something that's not yours to decide."

Get the name + title. CRM it.

"Call me back in six months."

"Absolutely. Quick question — is six months a specific trigger (like a contract renewal or a budget cycle), or just a general 'not now'? If it's a trigger I'll schedule for right before that."

"How did you get my number?"

"We pulled you from a public business directory. Nothing shady — I can tell you exactly where if you want. Is there anything about the outreach you'd want us to do differently?"

Answer honestly. If they ask to be removed, note in CRM + remove from list.
