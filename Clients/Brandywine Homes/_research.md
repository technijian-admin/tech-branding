## 02 — The Infill Growth Model (worth the model diagram)
Brandywine's business is a **deal pipeline**, not a retail funnel. The cycle:

1. **SOURCE** — find an underutilized infill parcel (church, motel, failed retail, strip mall, warehouse), assemble multi-owner lots, underwrite feasibility. *Gated by deal flow + land scarcity.* (Account-based: landowners, brokers.)
2. **ENTITLE** — navigate city planning, CEQA, zoning, by-right pathways, council/community approval; structure the public-private partnership. *Gated by entitlement time/risk.* (Account-based: municipalities.)
3. **BUILD** — develop a small/mid constrained infill site with a lean ~50-person team across several concurrent communities. *Gated by construction cost/schedule discipline.*
4. **SELL** — absorb the homes (attainable townhomes $480K–$800K; SFD up to $1M+) in a slow, incentive-heavy market. *Gated by absorption pace + marketing.* (Demand-gen: buyers.)

Plus a **capital layer** (lenders + equity partners) funding every deal. **Center of gravity = SOURCE + ENTITLE** (the supply side). That's where AI creates the most leverage and where NO competitor is using it.

## 03 — The Market & Where It's Going (2024–2026) — verified, citable
- **CA housing shortage still ~3M units**, yet **H1 2025 permits ~49,400 — lowest since 2014** (down ~3% YoY). SFR starts **−9%** in 2025; multifamily **+21%** — capital rotating to rental/BTR. **Inland Empire + Central Valley** is where supply still moves. (Davis Vanguard; firsttuesday)
- **For-sale squeeze:** new-home sales/community **−15% YoY**; unsold finished inventory **~2.6–2.7/community (+18% YoY)**; builder **incentives ~7.5% of base price** (rate buydowns). 30-yr fixed ~6.5%. **Burns Affordability Index ~39% (all-time high)**; **~62% more to own than rent** a 2-bed CA home. (HousingWire/John Burns; ManageCasa/C.A.R.)
- **The 2025 CEQA reform — biggest in 50+ yrs:** **AB 130 + SB 131 (signed June 30, 2025)** create a **statutory CEQA exemption for infill housing ≤20 acres** in urbanized areas. **ULI: cuts the entitlement timeline ~12–18 months.** (Holland & Knight; Urban Land/ULI) — *the single best executive-summary anchor for an infill builder.*
- **By-right toolbox:** **SB 35 → SB 423** (ministerial, 90–180 days; extended to 2036) · **SB 9** (duplex / lot split) · **AB 2011** (by-right housing on commercial land, no rezone, eff. 7/1/2023; AB 2243 2024 boosts to 70–80 du/ac) · **Density Bonus Law** (stacks) · **Builder's Remedy** (AB 1886 2024; most SoCal cities missed housing-element deadlines). *A builder fluent in stacking these — and able to screen/qualify parcels fast — can compress entitlement risk 12–18 months vs. competitors still running discretionary CEQA.*
- **Buyer journey is online-first:** **>70% of buyers use multiple platforms** (NAR); **NewHomeSource (Zonda) ~30M shoppers/yr, 1,500+ builders, 13,000+ communities.** Homes are researched and shortlisted before sales reps know the buyer exists.
