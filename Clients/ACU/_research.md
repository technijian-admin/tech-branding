## 03 — Industry Context (2025–2026, sourced)

- **The succession wave ("silver tsunami"):** ~**2.9M** US businesses owned by 55+; **~73%** of privately-held companies plan an ownership transition in the next decade (a **~$14T** opportunity, EPI); ~**49%** want out within 5 years. **The wedge = the planning gap:** only ~**19%** of boomers have started exit planning, ~**78%** lack a formal transition team, and **only 20–30% of businesses that go to market actually sell.** Acuity sells *preparedness + execution* into that gap. (Project Equity, Exit Planning Institute — advocacy/survey data, directional.)
- **Lower-middle-market M&A:** EV/EBITDA multiples ~**5.5–6.7×** in Acuity's deal zone ($1–25M); PE add-ons dominate LMM buying; 2026 = "steady, gradual reopening" as rate cuts narrow bid-ask spreads. Sell-side advisors are "non-negotiable" because owners have zero deal experience. (GF Data, Capstone, PwC/EY.)
- **ESOP tailwinds:** **6,609 ESOPs / $2T+ assets**; **309 new ESOPs in 2023**; the **WORK Act** (SECURE 2.0) created a DOL Employee Ownership Initiative; 2025 bills (Employee Ownership Representation Act passed Senate Oct 2025; S-corp ESOP expansion). DOL's stricter 2024 "adequate consideration" valuation rule was **withdrawn in 2025** — no final rule, so valuation rigor + defensibility matter more. (NCEO, congress.gov.)
- **How owners choose an advisor:** **CPA referrals are the #1 Center of Influence**; owners **self-educate online first** ("what is an ESOP," "how to sell my business," "business valuation") before engaging — a decade-long rise (EPI: formal exit-education 35%→68%; valuations 18%→60%, 2013→2023). **This is the AEO/content wedge.**
- **Regulatory/credential:** Federal **M&A-broker exemption** (EBITDA <$25M / rev <$250M) maps to Acuity's deals (but CA state registration + securities-sale deals still need the B-D/FINRA → Edgewater). **ESOP valuation = the #1 ESOP litigation flashpoint** (DOL scrutiny) → defensible, credentialed valuations are the product. **ASA mandates USPAP** (Kramer holds it).
